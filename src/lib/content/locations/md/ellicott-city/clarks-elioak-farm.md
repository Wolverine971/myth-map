## Overview

A 540-acre working family farm on Route 108 that doubles as the only place in Maryland to walk through the [salvaged characters of the old Enchanted Forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enchanted_Forest_%28Maryland%29) — Cinderella's pumpkin coach, the Three Bears' house, Little Red Riding Hood's path, and roughly 100 more fiberglass-and-concrete fairy tale figures that the Clark family rescued from the closed Route 40 amusement park between 2004 and 2015. Add a petting farm with goats, sheep, ponies, cows, and rabbits; a hayride; a small playground; a fairy-house scavenger hunt; and a seasonal pumpkin patch.

The sweet spot is **ages 1–6**, with diminishing returns after about 7 unless you hit a special event weekend. Kids under 1 are free but won't get much out of it. Skip this place if you're chasing thrills (it's slow-paced and a little dog-eared), if you can't stand sun (shade is genuinely scarce), or if a dusty-shoe day in the country isn't your speed.

## What to know before you go

- **Season:** **Open March 28 through November 8, 2026.** Closed in winter. Confirm the current season window on the [official farm site](https://clarklandfarm.com/) before driving.
- **Hours:** **Tuesday–Friday 10 a.m.–5 p.m., Saturday–Sunday 10 a.m.–5:30 p.m. Closed most Mondays**, with holiday-Monday exceptions (Memorial Day, Labor Day, and every Monday in October). During the late-August **Sunflower Showcase** the farm stays open until 7 p.m.
- **Admission:** **$8 per person age 1 and up. Babies under 1 are free.** That gets you the petting farm, Enchanted Forest, Fairy Forest, playground, and the picnic area. Cash, credit cards, and Apple Pay are accepted at the front gate.
- **Extras (each charged separately):** hay wagon ride $3, cow train $3, Enchanted Express train $5, pony ride $3, gem-mining flume $8–$15, bag of goat feed $1. Budget another $10–$20 per kid on top of admission if you plan to ride everything.
- **Cash matters:** the front gate and the Castle Store take cards; the **food stands and the fall pumpkin patch are cash only**. Bring small bills for goat feed too — $1s save a trip back to the store.
- **No online tickets.** It's a walk-up gate. No reservations are accepted for general admission.
- **Parking:** **Free, on-farm gravel lot.** It fills first on Saturdays and during pumpkin patch weekends — arrive by opening or after 2 p.m. for the easiest spot.
- **Restrooms:** **Porta-potties only**, with an outdoor handwashing station with running water and soap. No flushing toilets, no indoor restrooms, no dedicated nursing room, and no formal changing tables — plan to change a baby in the car.
- **Food policy:** **Outside food is welcome.** "Bring your lunch or a snack and enjoy a picnic" is the explicit invitation on the [Coming to the Farm page](https://clarklandfarm.com/coming_to_the_farm.html). Picnic tables sit in and around the maze. The Castle Store sells snacks, drinks, and ice cream; weekends often add a food truck.
- **What's not allowed:** **no dogs**, no smoking, no vaping, no alcohol, no drones.
- **Sun and shade:** there is **very little shade**. Bring sunscreen, hats, and water for everyone — the cow-train field and the Enchanted Forest paths are mostly open sun.
- **Strollers:** the main paths roll fine; the playground and a few Enchanted Forest spurs are uneven grass and gravel. A wagon does better than a delicate jogger.
- **Accessibility:** the farm is listed as handicap accessible by [Visit Howard County](https://www.visithowardcounty.com/listings/clarks-elioak-farm/28/) and main paths are level, but surfaces are gravel and grass — call ahead at **[(410) 730-4049](tel:4107304049)** if you have specific mobility questions.

## Tips for families

- **Bring a roll of singles.** Goat feed is $1 a bag, and you will buy three to four bags before lunch. The store is a long walk from the goat pen.
- **Skip Saturday 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. if you can.** That's birthday-party hour. Weekday mornings right at 10 a.m. open are the calmest 90 minutes you'll find here, and the goats are hungriest.
- **Hayride beats the cow train.** The hayride loops through the property and the kids spot fairy tale figures from the wagon; the cow train circles a dusty field behind a tractor. If your kid only has the patience for one tractor ride, pick the hayride.
- **Eat your picnic before the goats.** Goats are bold. Open snacks are fair game. Lock the cooler in the car and bring out food only at the picnic tables.
- **Wear closed shoes you don't mind losing.** This is a real farm. There is mud after rain and there are goat pellets. Sandals are a regret.
- **Hit the Fairy Forest scavenger hunt last.** Roughly 60 tiny fairy houses are hidden along the pine-tree path; it's the calmest activity on the farm and the perfect wind-down before the car.
- **The Enchanted Forest figures are photo-friendly but fragile.** The fiberglass figures are 60+ years old and show their age — kids can pose with them, not climb on them.
- **Go in late August for the [Sunflower Showcase](https://clarklandfarm.com/special_weekend_events.html).** Two acres of red and yellow sunflowers, extended hours until 7 p.m., the same $8 admission. Late-afternoon light is the move.
- **Princess and superhero weekends are a real thing.** Six scheduled character events run April through September; the [special weekend events page](https://clarklandfarm.com/special_weekend_events.html) is where to check before booking the day, because the farm noticeably fills up on those dates.
- **Plan 2 to 3 hours.** Less than that and you'll feel rushed past the rides; more than that and the under-4 crowd will be done. A 10 a.m. arrival with a picnic lunch at noon and a 12:45 p.m. exit is the textbook visit.
- **Save the gem-mining flume for last.** It's a sit-down activity at a sluice with running water — once you start, you're staying, and shoes will get wet.
- **Call ahead if the forecast is sketchy.** The farm doesn't refund admission for weather, and most of the experience is outdoors. **[(410) 730-4049](tel:4107304049)** or the [Facebook page](https://www.facebook.com/ClarksElioakFarm/) is where weather closures and weekend updates post first.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** **Right at 10 a.m. open** is the consistent best window — animals are hungry, no birthday-party buses yet, and the gravel lot is empty. Late afternoon after 3 p.m. on a weekday is the second-quiet window, especially in early fall.
- **Day of week:** **Tuesday through Friday are dramatically calmer than Saturday or Sunday.** Saturday in October is the busiest day of the year — pumpkin patch traffic stacks on top of Enchanted Forest traffic.
- **Season:** **Spring (baby animals, late March through May), late August (sunflowers), and the first half of October (pumpkin patch) are the standout windows.** Mid-summer is fine but hot, dusty, and shadeless. The farm is closed November 9 through late March — there is no winter visit option.
- **Weather contingency:** this is an outdoor-first farm. **Steady rain, high heat, and freezing cold are all visit-killers** — there is no indoor backup, the porta-potties are exposed, and the animals retreat. Save it for a temperate day.

## FAQs

**How much does it cost?**
Admission is $8 per person age 1 and up; babies under 1 are free. Rides and gem mining are extra ($3–$15 each), and a bag of goat feed is $1. Expect $10–$20 per kid in add-ons if you plan to ride the train, the hayride, or do pony rides.

**Is there parking?**
Yes — a free on-farm gravel lot. It fills first on Saturdays and during the fall pumpkin patch, so arrive at opening or after 2 p.m. for the easiest spot.

**Do I need a reservation?**
No, the farm is walk-up for general admission. There are **no online tickets**. Birthday parties, school field trips, and the [fall educational pumpkin tours](https://clarklandfarm.com/fall_educational_tours.html) do require advance booking — at least 72 hours ahead.

**How long should we plan for?**
Two to three hours is the standard visit. Less than two and you'll feel rushed; more than three and the under-4 set will be melting.

**What ages is this best for?**
Sweet spot is ages 1–6. Confident walkers through preschool age get the most out of the petting farm, the playground, and the Enchanted Forest figures. Older kids who like a slower pace can still enjoy the hayride and the fairy-house scavenger hunt, but tweens and teens will be bored.

**Can I bring snacks or a picnic?**
Yes — outside food is explicitly welcome, and there are picnic tables in the maze and the picnic area. The on-site Castle Store sells snacks, drinks, and ice cream if you forget. **Cash only at the food stands.**

**Are there bathrooms?**
**Porta-potties only**, with an outdoor handwashing station. There are no flushing toilets and no dedicated changing tables — plan to change a baby in the car.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Mostly yes. Main paths are flat and roll well for standard strollers and wagons. The playground area and a few Enchanted Forest spurs are uneven grass and gravel, so a chunky stroller or wagon handles better than a slim jogger.

**Are dogs allowed?**
No. The farm explicitly bans dogs (along with smoking, vaping, alcohol, and drones).

**Is it good in the rain?**
Not really. Almost everything is outdoors, the animals retreat in steady rain, and the porta-potties are exposed. The farm does not refund admission for weather. Check the [Facebook page](https://www.facebook.com/ClarksElioakFarm/) before you drive on a borderline day.

**Is it open year-round?**
No — the 2026 season runs **March 28 through November 8**. The farm is closed all winter; there is no off-season visit option.

**What's the deal with the Enchanted Forest?**
The original Enchanted Forest amusement park opened on U.S. Route 40 in 1955 and closed in 1995. Starting in 2004, the Clark family bought and moved roughly 100 of the storybook figures — including Cinderella's pumpkin coach and the Three Bears' house — to the farm. It's now the only place to see them in the wild. The [Wikipedia article on Clark's Elioak Farm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clark's_Elioak_Farm) has the full restoration history.

**Do they take credit cards?**
The front gate and the Castle Store take cards (and Apple Pay), but **food stands and the fall pumpkin patch are cash only**. Bring small bills.
