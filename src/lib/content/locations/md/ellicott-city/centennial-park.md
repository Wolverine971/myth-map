## Overview

Centennial Park wraps a 54-acre lake in 337 acres of Howard County parkland, and the thing most families come for is the loop: about 2.6 flat, paved, stroller-friendly miles circling the water, with a playground, a restroom, and a parking lot at each of the four entrances. It's free, open year-round, and on a decent-weather weekend it's the default "just get the kids outside" spot for a big chunk of Ellicott City.

The lake is the draw beyond the walk — seasonal boat rentals (paddle boats, kayaks, canoes, rowboats), a public boat ramp, and fishing stocked by the state. Swimming and paddleboarding are not allowed, so this is a look-and-paddle lake, not a wade-in one. Skip it as a rainy-day plan: it's almost entirely outdoors with no indoor backup, and long stretches of the loop bake in full sun on a hot afternoon.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Open daily, roughly 7 a.m. to sunset.
- **Cost:** Free to enter and free to park. You only pay for boat rentals and reserved pavilions.
- **Four entrances:** North, South, East, and West areas each have their own gate, parking lot, playground, and restroom. The South Area (10000 Clarksville Pike, off Route 108) is the main entrance with the most parking; North is at 9801 Old Annapolis Rd, West at 4651 Centennial Ln, East at 4800 Woodland Rd.
- **Winter access:** Only the South Area restroom stays open in winter, and East Area parking closes. Park at the South Area in the cold months.
- **Boat rentals (Adventure Shack):** Weekends from mid-April, then Wednesday–Sunday through the summer (June–August), then weekends again in September (plus Labor Day Monday) and October. Hours are 11 a.m.–7 p.m.; last boat out at 7 p.m., all boats back by 7:30. Call the status line at **410-313-7303** before you drive — hours flex with weather.
- **Boat prices:** Paddle boats (2- or 4-seat) and single/double kayaks and canoes run about $15/half-hour or hour for county residents ($17 non-resident); fishing kayaks ~$30 for up to three hours; a fishing boat with trolling motor ~$35 for up to four hours. Cash or card, no checks, plus a **$10 deposit and a physical photo ID per boat**. Paddle boats, kayaks, and canoes are half price on Wednesdays in June, July, and August.
- **Fishing:** The lake is stocked with rainbow trout (spring), largemouth bass, and catfish. Anglers 16 and up need a Maryland license; kids under 16 fish free, as does everyone on Maryland's free fishing days (the first two Saturdays in June and July 4).
- **Restrooms:** One at each of the four areas (South is the only one open in winter).
- **Stroller-friendliness:** The paved loop is flat and smooth — great for strollers, wagons, scooters, and balance bikes.
- **Accessibility:** The paved loop is wheelchair-accessible; the playgrounds include some inclusive equipment. Grassy and lakeshore edges are uneven.
- **Dogs:** Allowed on leash on the trails, but not in the playgrounds, picnic pavilions, or sports areas.
- **Food:** No reliable food on-site — pack your own water and snacks. There are picnic tables throughout, and pavilions you can reserve for parties.
- **What to bring:** Sunscreen and hats (shade is spotty on the loop), water, snacks, and cash-or-card plus a photo ID if you plan to rent a boat.

## Tips for families

- **Four playgrounds means a backup plan.** If the first lot is full and the playground is mobbed, drive to a different entrance — they're only minutes apart and the crowd thins fast away from the main South area.
- **The playgrounds aren't identical.** One is a bigger draw with larger climbing structures and fills up on weekends; the others are smaller, shadier, and calmer for younger kids. Worth trying a second entrance if the first feels overwhelming.
- **Shade is patchy on the loop.** Go early morning or late afternoon in summer — the lake breeze helps, but big sections of the paved path are fully exposed. Hats and sunscreen, not "we'll be fine."
- **Bring a physical ID for boat rentals.** You need a $10 deposit and a government photo ID for every boat, and the shack takes cash or card but no checks. Wednesdays in June–August are half price on paddle boats, kayaks, and canoes.
- **Call the boat status line first.** Rental hours (410-313-7303) shift with weather and staffing, and the whole operation closes for the season after October. Don't drive out for a paddle boat in April without checking.
- **Winter changes the rules.** Only the South Area restroom stays open and East parking closes, so use the South entrance (10000 Clarksville Pike) once it's cold.
- **Watch for goose mess in spring.** Canada geese nest along the water and get territorial, and the lawns near the lake collect droppings. Keep hands-in-mouth toddlers on the path and steer the stroller around it.
- **Pair it with Clarks Elioak Farm.** The storybook "Enchanted Forest" farm is just up Clarksville Pike — an easy half-day combo: burn energy on the lake loop, then go slow at the farm.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Early morning or late afternoon in summer for cooler temps, more shade, and easier parking. Midday on a warm weekend is the busiest window at the main playground.
- **Day of week:** Weekday mornings are quietest. Weekends in warm months are busy — but the park is so big it rarely feels packed once you're on the trail.
- **Season:** Spring through fall is the sweet spot. Boat rentals run mid-April to October, spring brings stocked trout, and the loop is a good fall-foliage walk. Winter keeps the trail open and the geese gathered on the lake, but boats are closed, most restrooms are shut, and East parking is gated.
- **Weather:** Fully outdoor with no indoor backup, so skip it in steady rain. Hot days need an early start and sun protection; cold days are fine for a bundled-up walk.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes — free lots at all four entrances (North, South, East, and West). The South Area off Route 108 has the most spaces and is the main entrance. In winter, East Area parking closes, so use the South Area.

**How much does it cost?**
The park is free to enter and free to park. The only costs are seasonal boat rentals and reserving a picnic pavilion.

**How long should we plan for?**
Anywhere from one to three hours. Walking the full lake loop takes about 45–60 minutes; add time for a playground, a picnic, or a boat rental and it easily fills a morning.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Yes. The roughly 2.6-mile loop around the lake is paved and mostly flat — one of the better stroller and wagon walks in the county.

**Can we swim or paddleboard?**
No. Swimming, stand-up paddleboarding, windsurfing, and tubing are all prohibited. You can rent a paddle boat, kayak, canoe, or rowboat, fish, or use the boat ramp.

**Do we need a fishing license?**
Anglers 16 and older need a Maryland fishing license. Kids under 16 fish for free, and no one needs a license on Maryland's free fishing days — the first two Saturdays in June and July 4.

**Are the boat rentals open year-round?**
No. The Adventure Shack runs weekends from mid-April, expands to Wednesday–Sunday for the summer, then tapers back to weekends through October before closing for the season. Call 410-313-7303 for the day's status before you go.

**Are dogs allowed?**
Yes, on a leash on the trails. Dogs are not allowed in the playgrounds, picnic pavilions, or sports areas.
