**Address:** 5685 Oakland Mills Rd, Columbia, MD 21045
**Website:** [https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks/parks-playgrounds-trails](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks/parks-playgrounds-trails)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Outdoor
**Price:** Free

## Overview

The North playground at Blandair Regional Park is "Laura's Place" — a large, fully fenced, ADA-compliant "Play-for-All" playground built so kids of every ability can play side by side. It's split into six themed zones: a farm/barn area for crawlers and babies (0–2), a dinosaur area for toddlers and preschoolers (2–5), an outer-space area and a second big-kid structure (both 5–12), plus an all-ages musical "free notes" garden and a treehouse boardwalk. The whole thing is surrounded by a fence with a self-locking double gate, which is the real selling point for parents of runners.

Sweet spot is roughly ages 1–10. There's genuinely something for a baby and a 9-year-old at the same time, which is rare. Skip it if you're hoping to tire out a tween or teen — the older-kid challenge course with turf and timers is in a separate East area of the park, not here, and it's built for ages 13+.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Open daily, roughly 7am to sunset (standard Howard County park hours). It's an outdoor playground with no gate-closing staff, but the park itself follows dawn-to-dusk rules.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Parking:** Large free lot right beside the North playground. It fills up on spring and summer weekends; weekday mornings you'll have your pick.
- **Restrooms:** A real restroom building sits next to the playground — you walk through the gate to reach it. Reviewers call it clean and note it's heated in cooler months.
- **Water:** Water fountains on site, but bring your own bottles for a long visit.
- **Stroller / wheelchair:** Fully accessible. Poured rubber and accessible surfacing throughout, wide paths, no required transfers. This is one of the better wheelchair- and stroller-friendly playgrounds in Howard County.
- **Shade:** Sun shades cover parts of the play areas, but large stretches are open turf and equipment that bakes in midday summer sun. Bring hats and sunscreen.
- **Food:** Picnic tables and benches throughout, plus a pavilion. No concessions — pack your own snacks and lunch.
- **What to bring:** Sunscreen, hats, water, and a second adult if you've got more than one little one (more on that below).

## Tips for families

- **Bring two adults if you have multiple under-5s.** The playground is huge and the zones spread out, so one kid bolts for the dinosaurs while another heads for the spaceship. Solo-parenting two toddlers here is a workout.
- **The double-gate fence is the whole point.** It's a self-closing, child-proof double gate — the safest playground in the area for a kid who runs. You can actually sit down for a minute.
- **Babies and crawlers go to the farm/barn zone.** It has lower structures, a butterfly-shaped shade canopy, and softer surfacing, away from the fast big-kid traffic.
- **Go on a weekday morning if you can.** Spring and summer weekends get packed; weekdays it's calm.
- **Park and enter at the North lot specifically.** Blandair has three separate play areas (North, West, and an East challenge course) at different entrances — North is Laura's Place, the big inclusive one. Don't accidentally aim for the West or East lots.
- **Summer afternoons get hot on the equipment.** Shade structures help, but plan a morning or late-afternoon visit in July and August and bring water.
- **The musical "free notes" garden is a good reset spot** when a younger sibling needs a break from the climbing structures.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Mornings are coolest and least crowded. In summer, avoid the midday sun on the open turf; come before 11am or after 4pm.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays beat weekends by a wide margin. Spring and summer Saturdays and Sundays draw real crowds.
- **Season:** Spring and fall are ideal — comfortable temperatures and full sun isn't a problem. It's usable on sunny winter days (bundle up; the restroom is heated). In steady rain it's a pass — there's no indoor option here.
- **Weather backup:** If rain rolls in, the nearby [Robinson Nature Center](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks/robinson-nature-center) makes a good indoor pivot.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes — a large free lot sits right next to the North playground. It can fill on spring and summer weekends, but weekday parking is easy.

**Is the playground fenced?**
Yes. Laura's Place is fully fenced with a self-closing, child-proof double gate at the entrance, which is a major draw for parents of young runners.

**What ages is it good for?**
Roughly 1 to 10. There are dedicated zones for ages 0–2, 2–5, and two areas for 5–12, plus all-ages musical and treehouse sections. Older tweens and teens will be bored — the 13+ challenge course is in a separate East area of the park.

**Is it stroller- and wheelchair-friendly?**
Yes. It's an ADA "Play-for-All" playground with accessible surfacing, wide paths, and no required transfers, so strollers and wheelchairs roll throughout.

**Are there bathrooms?**
Yes. A restroom building is next to the playground, reachable through the gate. Reviewers describe it as clean and note it's heated in cold weather.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes. There are picnic tables, benches, and a pavilion. There's no concession stand, so pack snacks, lunch, and water.

**How long should we plan for?**
Most families spend 1.5 to 3 hours. The size and variety of zones easily fill a morning, especially with kids of different ages.

**Is there shade on hot days?**
Partly. Sun shades cover sections of the play areas, but a lot of the equipment and turf is exposed and gets hot midday in summer. Bring hats, sunscreen, and water, and aim for a morning visit.
