## Overview

The West Area playground at Blandair Regional Park is the park's smaller, shadier play spot — a single compact structure on artificial turf with a large shade canopy overhead, set right beside the synthetic turf sports fields and bleachers. The equipment is relatively new and well kept, and the shade cover makes it one of the more comfortable playgrounds in the area on a hot afternoon. It's a different animal from the North Area's giant fenced inclusive playground (Laura's Place); this side is quicker, calmer, and built for a shorter stop.

What changed everything here: Phase 6 opened on June 5, 2026, adding a 15,000-square-foot destination [skatepark](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/News090424), six lighted pickleball courts, two lighted basketball courts, and more than 100 new parking spaces — all in the West Area. So this is now the side to come to if you've got a skater or scooter kid, or a mixed-age crew where one wants the playground and an older sibling wants the skatepark or a court. If you only want the marquee playground experience, head to the North Area instead; the West playground alone won't fill a whole outing for a single school-age kid.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Open daily 7am to sunset (standard Howard County park hours). No staffed gate. The new basketball and pickleball courts are lighted, so they're usable into the evening within park hours.
- **Cost:** Free — playground, skatepark, and courts. No registration card or reservation needed for drop-in skating.
- **Entrance:** Use the West Area entrance at 5750 Oakland Mills Road. GPS can misroute near the park; follow the Oakland Mills Road signage rather than trusting the pin, and aim for the West lot specifically (North is Laura's Place, East is the teen challenge course).
- **Parking:** Free lot, and Phase 6 added 100+ spaces here to handle athletic events. On a normal day parking is easy; expect it to fill when there's a tournament on the turf fields or a busy stretch at the new skatepark.
- **Restrooms:** A restroom building serves the West Area. (Confirm changing-table availability on arrival — the North Area restroom by Laura's Place has an adult changing table.)
- **Stroller / wheelchair:** Paved pathways connect the West Area, and the playground sits on firm artificial turf, so a standard stroller rolls fine. It's not the fully ADA "Play-for-All" build that Laura's Place is in the North Area — if you need certified accessible play surfacing and zero transfers, go North.
- **Shade:** The playground's large shade canopy is the standout feature. The turf fields, courts, and skatepark, though, are wide open and bake in midday summer sun. Bring hats, sunscreen, and water.
- **Food:** Picnic tables and benches in the area; no concession stand you can count on. Pack your own snacks, lunch, and water.
- **Skatepark gear:** Bring your own helmet and pads. The county encouraged riders to bring helmet and pads for the opening, and helmets are the norm at public skateparks — don't expect loaner gear.

## Tips for families

- **Come here for the mixed-age split.** One adult takes a toddler to the shaded playground while another shadows a school-age kid at the skatepark or a basketball court — the West Area now does both within sight of each other.
- **The shade canopy is the reason to pick West over North on a hot day.** The big play structure is covered, so the equipment doesn't turn into a griddle the way open turf does.
- **It gets busy during games.** The playground sits next to the turf sports fields; when there's a tournament or league game, both the lot and the playground fill with siblings of players. Weekday mornings with no game scheduled are calmest.
- **The skatepark and courts are brand new (opened June 5, 2026).** If you're making a special trip for them, it's worth a quick check of the county page or social for any opening-period closures or events before you load the car.
- **Helmets on for the skatepark.** It's a real 15,000-square-foot concrete park built for multiple skill levels, not a beginner ramp — bring a helmet and pads, and walk a young first-timer through the lines before they drop in.
- **Park once, use the paved paths.** The connecting pathways let you reach the playground, courts, and skatepark from one lot without moving the car.
- **For the big inclusive playground, go North, not here.** If your kid specifically wants Laura's Place — the fenced, themed, ADA playground — that's the North Area at a different entrance off Oakland Mills Road.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Mornings are coolest and least crowded. In summer, the shaded playground holds up better midday than most, but the courts and skatepark are exposed — ride early or after 4pm. Lighted courts make evening play an option.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays beat weekends, especially when the turf fields host games or tournaments. Check whether a sporting event is scheduled before a weekend visit.
- **Season:** Spring and fall are ideal. Sunny winter days work if you bundle up. In steady rain it's a pass — everything here is outdoors.
- **Weather backup:** If rain moves in, the nearby [Robinson Nature Center](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks/robinson-nature-center) on Cedar Lane is a solid indoor pivot.

## FAQs

**Where do I park?**
Use the West Area lot at 5750 Oakland Mills Road. Phase 6 added more than 100 spaces here, so parking is usually easy — but it fills during games on the turf fields or busy stretches at the new skatepark.

**Is this the big playground at Blandair?**
No. The West playground is the smaller, shaded one by the sports fields. The large fenced inclusive playground, Laura's Place, is in the North Area at a separate entrance.

**What ages is the West playground good for?**
It's a single compact structure, best for toddlers through early elementary. Older kids will get more out of the new skatepark and basketball courts than the playground itself.

**Is there a skatepark, and does it cost anything?**
Yes — a 15,000-square-foot concrete skatepark opened in the West Area on June 5, 2026. It's free with no registration card required. Bring your own helmet and pads.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Yes. Paved paths connect the area and the playground sits on firm artificial turf, so a standard stroller works. For certified ADA play surfacing, Laura's Place in the North Area is the better fit.

**Are there bathrooms?**
Yes, a restroom building serves the West Area. Check changing-table availability on arrival.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes. There are picnic tables and benches, and no reliable concession stand, so pack snacks, lunch, and water.

**Is there shade on hot days?**
The playground has a large shade canopy, which is its best feature. The turf fields, courts, and skatepark are open and get hot midday — bring hats, sunscreen, and water.

**How long should we plan for?**
For the playground alone, 45 minutes to an hour. Add the skatepark or courts for an older kid and you can stretch it to a half-day.
