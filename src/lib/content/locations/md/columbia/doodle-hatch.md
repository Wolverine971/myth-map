# Doodle Hatch

**Address:** 8775 Cloudleap Court Columbia, MD 21045
**Website:** [https://www.doodlehatch.com/](https://www.doodlehatch.com/)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Indoor
**Price:** $

## Overview

DoodleHATCH is an interactive art museum built inside a former grocery store in Long Reach Village Center, set up as a make-believe "department store" for creatures from fairy tales and myths. Founder Lee Andersen and teams of artists turned the space into a series of touch-everything rooms: a unicorn grooming salon (Mane Salon), an Intergalactic Space Station with switches and buttons to flip, a Giant's Bed you climb onto, a witch's kitchen, a glowing Stargate entrance, and a quiet reading nook. The whole point is hands-on imaginative play and getting kids off screens — staff actively want children touching, role-playing, and making noise.

It's aimed at ages 2 to 102, but the sweet spot is roughly 2 through 9. Toddlers and elementary kids who like dress-up and pretend play get the most out of it. Tweens and teens tend to do a quick loop and lose interest, so skip it if your kids have aged out of imaginative play. One real caution: the Stargate entrance uses laser/strobe lighting that could trigger photosensitive epilepsy — worth knowing before you walk in.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Friday–Sunday, 10am–5pm, with last entry at 3pm (closed Mon–Thu). Hours have expanded to weekdays during past summers — confirm on their site or Facebook before a weekday trip.
- **Admission:** $10 per person. Children 2 and under are free. Tickets are nonrefundable but rescheduling is free.
- **Visit length:** Admission covers a timed 2-hour visit, which is plenty for most families.
- **Tickets:** Buy online ahead on weekends and holidays; a health-and-safety waiver is required before entry, so fill it out in advance to save time at the door.
- **Bring socks.** Socks are required to climb on the Giant's Bed — pack a pair for every kid (and yourself if you want to join).
- **Supervision:** Everyone under 18 must be with an adult 18 or older. This is a play-alongside place, not a drop-off.
- **Parking:** Free surface lot at Long Reach Village Center, right outside the door.
- **What's included:** Each child gets a free book to take home.
- **Indoor and climate-controlled** — a solid rainy-day or too-hot/too-cold-day pick.

## Tips for families

- Pack socks for everyone before you leave the house — kids who forget can't use the Giant's Bed, and there's nothing sadder than watching from the side.
- Complete the waiver online before you arrive so you're not filling out forms with an antsy toddler in tow.
- Book a weekend morning slot online. The 10am opening is the calmest stretch; afternoons and the run-up to the 3pm last entry get busier.
- Two hours is the built-in window — frame it for younger kids as "we'll do every room twice" so the timed exit doesn't land as a surprise meltdown.
- If anyone in your family has photosensitive epilepsy, walk past the Stargate's laser/strobe entrance with care or ask staff about an alternate path.
- Leave the stroller parked once you're inside. The fun is climbing, flipping switches, and dressing up — your kid won't be in the seat, and most installations aren't stroller activities.
- It's an active, hands-on space, so dress kids in play clothes they can move and climb in.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Right at the 10am open is quietest. Aim to arrive early; last entry is 3pm and the back half of the day is the busiest.
- **Day of week:** Saturdays and Sundays draw the biggest crowds and birthday parties. If summer weekday hours are running, a weekday visit is far calmer.
- **Season:** Indoor and climate-controlled, so it's a year-round option — especially good on rainy, sweltering, or freezing days when outdoor plans fall through.
- **Weather:** No weather contingency needed; the entire experience is inside.
