## Overview

Playseum is an indoor pretend-play space inside [The Mall in Columbia](https://www.mallincolumbia.com/) with 20+ themed "Cityshops" — a grocery store, vet, bakery, barber shop, art studio, science bar, and a Pet Shop with real animals (bunnies, bearded dragons, birds). It's the Columbia outpost of the [Be With Me Playseum](https://www.playseum.com/) chain that started in Bethesda in 2006 and is built around unstructured, parent-with-kid imaginative play rather than a big-bouncer kid-drop-off scene.

Sweet spot is **roughly ages 2–8**. Toddlers love the costumes and the live animals; pre-readers and early-elementary kids will run from shop to shop for a couple of hours. Skip it if your kid is older than 8 or 9 — they'll be bored — and skip it on a rainy Saturday afternoon if you can't handle crowds, since this is the obvious "what do we do today" spot for half of Howard County when the weather is bad.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Mon–Thu 10am–5pm, Fri–Sat 10am–7pm, Sun 1pm–6pm.
- **Admission:** $15 per person, ages 1–100. Under 1 is free. Wristband is good all day, so you can leave for lunch and come back.
- **Tuesdays are cheap for grandparents:** Grandparents Tuesdays admission is $2 for accompanying grandparents.
- **Creation Stations cost extra.** The Art Bar, Soap Bar, Bakery Bar, and Science Bar projects run $3–$30 (most are around $5) and aren't included with admission. Bring cash or expect to load "Playseum Dollars" at the front desk.
- **Pet Shop petting is hourly.** Staff brings out the animals for handling at the top of each hour — plan around it if that's the thing your kid wants.
- **Socks required** for the play areas. Bring a pair; they sell them at the front if you forget.
- **Parking:** Free in any Mall in Columbia lot or garage. Easiest entry is the parking garage by Nordstrom — Playseum is a short indoor walk from there.
- **Food:** No on-site café. You can bring snacks and eat in the designated area, or step out to the mall food court (a few minutes' walk) without losing your wristband.
- **Bathrooms:** Mall restrooms are the closest. Changing tables in the family restrooms.
- **Stroller-friendliness:** The Cityshops are tight quarters. There's a small stroller parking spot near the entrance — leave it there and carry a baby in a carrier, or park the stroller in the mall corridor.
- **No reservations needed** for general admission. Birthday parties need to be booked by phone.

## Tips for families

- **Go on a weekday morning if you can.** Saturdays after 11am are the rough hours — birthday parties pile up and the popular shops get crowded fast.
- **Pre-load a few "Playseum Dollars" at the desk** before your kid spots the Bake Shop. Cupcake decorating, slime, and soap-making are the most-requested upgrades and they add up if you decide one project at a time.
- **Time your visit to the hourly Pet Shop slot.** A 10:15am arrival means a long wait; arriving five minutes before the top of the hour gets you front-row on the rabbit handling.
- **Bring labeled water bottles.** There's no on-site café, and refilling at the mall food court means breaking the play streak.
- **Pack a change of clothes for messy ages.** Soap-making and cupcake decorating leave a mark; the art station is washable but real paint shows up at home later.
- **If you have a baby in tow, bring a carrier**, not a stroller. You'll thank yourself in the narrow Cityshops.
- **Older sibling? Pair with [Playseum Upstairs](https://www.playseum.com/playseum-upstairs)** — a separate space in the same mall geared toward tweens and teens. It's run by the same operator and worth a look if your group spans ages.
- **Don't expect a quiet break for yourself.** Playseum is designed around parents playing _with_ the kid, not next to them on a phone. If you want a coffee-and-watch setup, this isn't it.

## Best time to visit

Weekday mornings right after open (10am) are the calmest stretch — toddler crowd, parking is empty, no party groups yet. Friday after-school and all day Saturday are the busiest; Sunday afternoons sit in the middle.

It's an indoor space inside a mall, so it works year-round and especially earns its keep on rainy or single-digit days when the local playgrounds are out. Summer mornings before pool weather kicks in are another sweet spot. The themed weekday programming (Marvelous Mondays storytime, Wacky Wednesdays soap-making, Think Big Thursdays science projects, Fun Fridays crafts) is included with admission and worth timing around if your kid loves any of those.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes, free in any Mall in Columbia lot or garage. The Nordstrom-side parking garage is the closest indoor route. Spots fill up on Saturdays but the garage is big.

**How long should we plan for?**
Most families spend 2–3 hours. The wristband is good all day, so you can leave for lunch in the food court and come back without paying again.

**What's the right age for Playseum?**
Roughly ages 2 through 8. Two- and three-year-olds love the costumes and animals; school-age kids dig into the Creation Stations. Kids 9 and up tend to lose interest unless they're tagging along for a younger sibling.

**Can I bring my own snacks?**
Yes. There's a designated eating area inside, and snacks/water bottles are welcome. The mall food court is also a few minutes' walk if you want a real meal.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Partial. The Cityshops are narrow and most parents end up parking the stroller near the entrance. A baby carrier works better inside the play space. The mall walk from the parking garage is fully stroller-accessible.

**Are socks required?**
Yes — for hygiene in the play areas. Bring a pair for everyone in your group, or buy them at the desk.

**Do we need reservations?**
No for general admission, just walk in. Yes for birthday parties — call 240-507-8965 to book a party slot.

**Is everything included in the $15 admission?**
General play in the Cityshops, the Pet Shop, and the daily themed activity are all included. The Art, Soap, Bakery, and Science Bar projects cost an extra $3–$30 (most are around $5) and are purchased with Playseum Dollars at the front desk.

**Is it good in the rain?**
Yes — this is one of the better rainy-day spots in Howard County. It's fully indoor and connected to mall food and bathrooms.

**Is there a place to change a baby?**
Yes — changing tables in the mall's family restrooms just outside the Playseum entrance. There isn't a dedicated nursing room inside the venue itself.
