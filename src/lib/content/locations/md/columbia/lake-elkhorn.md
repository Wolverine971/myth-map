# Lake Elkhorn

**Address:** 6800 Cradlerock Way Columbia, MD 21046
**Website:** [https://www.visithowardcounty.com/listings/lake-elkhorn/8613/](https://www.visithowardcounty.com/listings/lake-elkhorn/8613/)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Outdoor
**Price:** Free

## Overview

Lake Elkhorn is a man-made lake in the Owen Brown village of Columbia, ringed by a flat, paved two-mile loop path that's one of the easiest stroller walks in Howard County. It's free, run by the [Columbia Association](https://columbiaassociation.org/open-space/lake-parks/), and the draw is simple: a calm lake walk with a playground, a fishing dock, and a lot of ducks, geese, herons, and turtles to point at along the way.

This is a low-key outdoor outing, not a destination with a lot of bells and whistles. The big thing to know up front: there are **no public restrooms** (the only bathrooms belong to the rentable pavilion), there's **no swimming**, and there are **no boat or kayak rentals here** — those are at the downtown Columbia Lakefront on Lake Kittamaqundi. Come for a shady loop, a playground stop, and some dock fishing. Skip it if you've got just-potty-trained kids who can't make it through a walk without a bathroom, or if you were hoping to rent a paddleboat.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Dawn-to-dusk passive park; the loop is open daily and free. The path has lighting only near the dam and road crossings, not the full loop, so plan to be done by dark.
- **Cost:** Free. No admission, no parking fee.
- **Parking:** Two free lots. The **7200 Dockside Lane** lot (north shore) is closest to the playground and the boat dock — use this one with kids. A second lot sits at **9705 Broken Land Parkway** near the pavilion end. Lots fill on warm weekends; if Dockside is full you can park along Cradlerock Way near the playground.
- **The loop:** About 2 miles, paved, mostly flat with a few gentle grades, with some boardwalk sections. Roughly a 45–60 minute walk at a kid's pace.
- **Restrooms:** None open to the public. The pavilion has bathrooms but only during rentals. The nearest public option reviewers point to is the McDonald's across Cradlerock Way. Plan accordingly.
- **Playground:** Near the north-shore/Dockside side, with separate equipment for ages 2–5 and 5–12, baby and big-kid swings, a sandbox, and spring riders. It's usually shady.
- **Fishing:** Allowed from shore and the dock. Anglers 16+ need a Maryland non-tidal freshwater fishing license (about $32/year for residents); under 16 fish free. The dock has no railings — keep little ones within arm's reach.
- **Boating:** Bring your own — canoes, kayaks, or rowboats up to 16 feet, with a free Columbia Association boat permit/sticker. **No motorized craft (gas or electric), no paddleboats or paddleboards.** No swimming or wading, either.
- **Food:** No concessions. Bring a picnic; there are benches and grassy spots, plus the pavilion if you rent it.
- **Stroller-friendly:** Yes — the paved loop rolls the whole way.
- **Dogs:** Leashed dogs welcome; clean up after them.
- **What to bring:** Water, snacks, sunscreen for the open stretches, and a plan for bathrooms.

## Tips for families

- **Park at the Dockside Lane lot.** It puts you right by the playground and the dock, so you're not walking half the loop before the kids get to play.
- **Go to the bathroom before you arrive.** This is the single most common complaint about the park — there are no public restrooms. The McDonald's across Cradlerock Way is the backup.
- **Watch the dock.** The fishing dock has no railings, so it's a close-supervision zone for toddlers, even if you're not fishing.
- **Watch your step near the shoreline.** Resident geese leave the grass and paths messy, especially near the water — stroller wheels and toddler hands find it fast.
- **Make it a loop-and-play, not a forced march.** Two miles is a lot for short legs. Many families do the playground plus a half-loop to the dam and back rather than the full circuit.
- **Bring a kid's fishing rod in spring.** The lake is stocked with trout in spring and holds bluegill, crappie, and largemouth bass — easy bites for first-time anglers off the dock.
- **Pack everything you'll need.** No concessions, no water fountains you can count on, no restrooms. Treat it like a trailhead, not a facility.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Weekday mornings are quietest and coolest. Warm-weather weekend afternoons are when parking gets tight.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays beat weekends for parking and elbow room.
- **Season:** Spring (mild weather, trout stocking, goslings on the water) and fall (foliage around the wooded loop) are the sweet spots. Summer works but the open stretches near the dam and pavilion get hot, and the geese are out in force.
- **Weather:** Fully outdoor with no rain cover unless you've rented the pavilion — skip it in steady rain. Winter walking is fine on milder days; the open water below the dam draws ducks when the rest of the lake freezes.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes, two free lots. The 7200 Dockside Lane lot is closest to the playground and dock; a second lot is at 9705 Broken Land Parkway near the pavilion. Both fill on warm weekends, so arrive early or park along Cradlerock Way near the playground.

**Are there restrooms?**
No public restrooms. The pavilion has bathrooms but only for renters. Use the bathroom before you come; reviewers point to the McDonald's across Cradlerock Way as the nearest option.

**How long should we plan for?**
Anywhere from one to two and a half hours. The full paved loop is about 45–60 minutes of walking, plus time at the playground or dock. With young kids, a playground stop and a half-loop is plenty.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Yes. The roughly two-mile loop is paved and mostly flat with a few gentle grades and some boardwalk, so a standard stroller rolls the whole way.

**Can we go swimming or rent a kayak?**
No on both. Swimming and wading are prohibited in all Columbia Association lakes, and there are no boat rentals here — you'd bring your own registered boat. Seasonal kayak and paddleboat rentals are at the downtown Columbia Lakefront on Lake Kittamaqundi instead.

**Can the kids fish?**
Yes, from shore or the dock. Maryland requires a freshwater fishing license for anglers 16 and up; kids under 16 fish free. The lake has bluegill, crappie, largemouth bass, and spring-stocked trout. Note the dock has no railings.

**Is it good for toddlers?**
Yes — the flat loop is easy for new walkers and strollers, and the playground has dedicated 2–5 equipment, a sandbox, and baby swings. The main catch is the no-restroom situation and the unrailed dock.

**Are dogs allowed?**
Yes, on a leash, as long as you clean up after them.

**Is there shade?**
A fair amount. The loop is wooded through many sections and the playground is usually shady, but the stretches near the dam and the pavilion are open and sunny.
