# Cedar Lane Park Playground

**Address:** 5081 Cedar Ln, Columbia, MD 21044
**Website:** [https://www.howardcountymd.gov/CedarLanePark](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/CedarLanePark)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Outdoor
**Price:** Free

## Overview

Cedar Lane Park is a 93-acre Howard County park split into a West and an East area, connected by a 0.8-mile paved path through the woods. This page covers the **West side**, at 5081 Cedar Lane — the bigger sports half, with four lighted ball diamonds, two lighted synthetic turf fields, sand volleyball, bocce, horseshoe pits, an indoor fitness facility, restrooms, a pavilion, and the playground. It's free and run by [Howard County Recreation & Parks](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/CedarLanePark).

The playground has slides, climbing structures, and swings, with outdoor fitness equipment alongside for parents who want to move while the kids play. Plan around the park's real identity, though: this is one of Howard County's busiest sports complexes, so the playground works best as a weekday or between-games stop, not a destination outing. If you want a big showpiece playground, Blandair Regional Park or Centennial Park are stronger picks; come here for an easy, uncrowded play stop plus a stroller-friendly woods walk.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Sunrise to sunset daily (posted as roughly 7am to dusk).
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Parking:** Free lots on site. The lots serve the ball diamonds and turf fields first, so game and tournament days — spring and fall weekends especially — fill them fast.
- **Restrooms:** On site in the West area, plus water fountains. Reviewers report the fountains are unreliable, so bring your own water.
- **Playground:** Slides, climbing structures, and swings, with outdoor fitness equipment nearby.
- **Stroller-friendly:** Yes — paved paths throughout, including the 0.8-mile wooded path to the East side.
- **Food:** No concessions. Picnic tables and a first-come, first-served pavilion (reservable through [Recreation & Parks](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks), 410-313-4700) make it easy to bring your own.
- **Dogs:** Allowed on leash in the park, but not in the playground, picnic, or sports areas.
- **What to bring:** Water bottles, sunscreen, and a ball or scooter for the open space.
- **Weather line:** 410-313-4453 for field and program status.

## Tips for families

- **Time your visit around the sports schedule.** Weekend mornings in soccer and baseball season bring tournament crowds and full lots. Weekday mornings and early afternoons, you often get the playground nearly to yourselves.
- **Walk the connecting path.** The 0.8-mile paved path between the West and East areas runs through shaded woods — an easy stroller loop or first bike-ride territory, and you get a second playground at the far end.
- **Use the fitness equipment.** The outdoor fitness stations sit by the playground, so one parent gets a workout while keeping eyes on the slide line.
- **Bring your own water.** The fountains have a spotty reputation in reviews; don't count on them on a hot day.
- **Make it a doubleheader.** Kids who burn out on the playground recover on the open grass or watching a few innings on the diamonds — games are free entertainment.
- **Lighted fields buy you evening time.** The ball diamonds and turf fields are lighted, so fall evening visits stay lively later than at most parks — but the park itself still closes at dusk for general use.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Weekday mornings are quietest. Late afternoon works on non-game days.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays beat weekends by a wide margin — this is a tournament-ready sports complex, and weekend field bookings drive the crowds and parking.
- **Season:** Spring and fall are the sweet spot for weather, but they're also peak sports season; go midweek. Winter visits work on milder days since the park stays open year-round.
- **Weather:** Fully outdoor playground with no rain cover — skip it in steady rain. The indoor fitness facility hosts scheduled Rec & Parks programs, not drop-in play, so it isn't a rainy-day backup.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes, free lots on site. They fill during weekend games and tournaments on the ball diamonds and turf fields, so arrive early on spring and fall weekends.

**Is the playground good for toddlers?**
Yes — the slides, low climbing features, and swings cover toddlers through elementary ages. It's a neighborhood-grade playground, so expect 45 minutes to an hour of play rather than an all-morning destination.

**Are there restrooms?**
Yes, the West area has restrooms and water fountains. Bring your own water as a backup — reviewers report the fountains don't always work.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Yes. Paved paths run through the park, and the 0.8-mile wooded path to the East area is paved the whole way.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes. There are picnic tables and a pavilion that's first-come, first-served unless someone has reserved it. There's no concession stand.

**Are dogs allowed?**
On leash, yes — but not in the playground, picnic, or sports areas, and you're required to clean up.

**What's the difference between the West and East areas?**
The West side (5081 Cedar Lane) has the lighted ball diamonds, turf fields, volleyball, bocce, horseshoe pits, the indoor fitness facility, and a playground with fitness equipment. The East side (10745 Route 108) has tennis and basketball courts, grass fields, and its own playground. The paved path connects them in about a 15–20 minute stroller walk.

**How long should we plan for?**
About an hour for the playground alone. Stretch it to a half-day by walking the path to the East playground and packing a picnic.
