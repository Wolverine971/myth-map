## Overview

"Bland Air South" is the older name for the developed, active-recreation half of Blandair Regional Park — the part that sits on the south side of MD-175, where all the park's playgrounds and sports fields actually are. The 300-acre park's north 200 acres are kept as nature preserve (forest, wetland, meadow); the play equipment, turf fields, and pavilions are all on this southern core, reached from Oakland Mills Road.

There are two playgrounds to know here. The big one is **Laura's Place**, a fully fenced, ADA "Play-for-All" playground in the North Area with six themed zones (farm, dinosaur, outer space, plus a musical garden and treehouse boardwalk). The second is a smaller, standard playground beside the synthetic turf fields in the **West Area**. Sweet spot is roughly ages 1–10. Skip a special trip if you're trying to wear out a tween or teen — the closest thing for them is the free outdoor Challenge Course in the East Area, built for ages 13+.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Open daily sunrise to sunset (standard Howard County park hours, posted as roughly 7am–dusk). No staffed gate; the park follows dawn-to-dusk rules.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Entrance:** Use Oakland Mills Road — that's where the signed entrances and parking lots are (North, West, and East areas). GPS sometimes misroutes near the park; follow the Oakland Mills Road signage at the roundabout rather than trusting the pin.
- **Parking:** Free lots at each area, including 140+ spaces added by the North playground. Lots fill on spring and summer weekends; weekday mornings are easy.
- **Restrooms:** Real restroom buildings in the North, West, and East areas. The North restrooms (by Laura's Place) include an adult changing table and sit just outside the playground fence.
- **Stroller / wheelchair:** Fully accessible at Laura's Place — poured rubber and accessible surfacing, wide paved paths, no required transfers. Paved pathways connect the areas, so strollers roll between them.
- **Shade:** Sun shades cover parts of Laura's Place, and the West playground has a shade canopy, but large stretches are open turf that bakes midday in summer. Bring hats and sunscreen.
- **Food:** Picnic tables, benches, and pavilions throughout. No concessions — pack your own snacks, lunch, and water.
- **Construction note:** A Phase 6 build-out in the West Area (a destination skatepark, lighted pickleball and basketball courts, more parking) broke ground in September 2024. Expect active construction and possible path or lot closures on the West side; check the county page before relying on the West playground.

## Tips for families

- **Aim for Laura's Place in the North Area, not the pin on Sealed Message Rd.** The fenced inclusive playground is the real destination; enter from Oakland Mills Road where the parking is.
- **The double-gate fence at Laura's Place is the whole point.** Self-closing, child-proof double gates make it the safest playground in the area for a kid who bolts — you can actually sit for a minute.
- **Bring two adults for multiple under-5s.** The zones spread out, so one kid heads for the dinosaurs while another runs for the spaceship. Solo-parenting two toddlers here is a workout.
- **The West playground is the quieter backup.** Smaller and usually less crowded than Laura's Place — handy on a packed Saturday, though Phase 6 construction may affect access.
- **Go weekday mornings.** Spring and summer weekends draw real crowds, especially during summer-camp season; weekdays are calm.
- **Summer afternoons get hot on the equipment.** Shade structures help, but plan a morning or late-afternoon visit in July and August and bring water.
- **Use the paved paths to combine areas.** The connecting pathways are stroller-friendly, so you can park once and walk between the playground, fields, and pavilions.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Mornings are coolest and least crowded. In summer, beat the midday sun on the open turf — come before 11am or after 4pm.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays beat weekends by a wide margin. Spring and summer weekends get busy.
- **Season:** Spring and fall are ideal. It's usable on sunny winter days (bundle up; the North restroom is heated). In steady rain it's a pass — there's no indoor option here.
- **Weather backup:** If rain rolls in, the nearby [Robinson Nature Center](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks/robinson-nature-center) on Cedar Lane makes a good indoor pivot.

## FAQs

**Where do I actually park?**
Use the Oakland Mills Road entrances — that's where the signed lots are for the North, West, and East areas. The "Sealed Message Rd" address sits on a residential street backing the park's southern edge, not at a parking lot, so don't rely on it as a destination.

**Is there a separate "south" playground?**
Not as its own facility. Blandair's playgrounds all sit on the developed south side of the park (south of MD-175): the big inclusive playground, Laura's Place, in the North Area, and a smaller playground in the West Area. The county organizes them as North, West, and East areas.

**Is the playground fenced?**
Laura's Place is fully fenced with a self-closing, child-proof double gate at the entrance, which is a major draw for parents of young runners. The West playground is a standard, open layout.

**What ages is it good for?**
Roughly 1 to 10. Laura's Place has zones for ages 0–2, 2–5, and two for 5–12, plus all-ages musical and treehouse sections. Older tweens and teens will be bored — the 13+ Challenge Course is in the East Area.

**Is it stroller- and wheelchair-friendly?**
Yes. Laura's Place is an ADA "Play-for-All" playground with accessible surfacing, wide paths, and no required transfers, and paved pathways connect the park's areas.

**Are there bathrooms?**
Yes — restroom buildings in the North, West, and East areas. The North restrooms by Laura's Place include an adult changing table and sit just outside the playground fence.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes. There are picnic tables, benches, and pavilions. There's no concession stand, so pack snacks, lunch, and water.

**How long should we plan for?**
Most families spend 1.5 to 3 hours. Laura's Place alone can fill a morning, especially with kids of different ages.

**Is there shade on hot days?**
Partly. Shade structures cover parts of the playgrounds, but a lot of the equipment and turf is exposed and gets hot midday in summer. Bring hats, sunscreen, and water, and aim for a morning visit.
