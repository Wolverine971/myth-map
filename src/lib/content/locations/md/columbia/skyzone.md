# Skyzone

**Address:** 7175 Oakland Mills Rd Columbia, MD 21046
**Website:** [https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Indoor
**Price:** $$

## Overview

A 30,000-square-foot trampoline park off Oakland Mills Road with the standard Sky Zone lineup: a wall-to-wall freestyle court, a foam pit, a [SkySlam](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/attractions/skyslam/) basketball lane, dodgeball courts, an iWall climbing wall, a Speed Zone running track, and a fenced [Little Ninja](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/attractions/toddler-zone/) area for kids who aren't ready for the open court. Friday nights run as GLOW with blacklight, lasers, and music.

Sweet spot is **ages 5 through about 12**, with the toddler zone making it tolerable for under-5s if you stick to off-peak hours. Skip it if your kid is under 3 and easily overwhelmed by noise — the open court gets loud, fast — or if you're hoping a single 60-minute ticket will tire out a 9-year-old. Two hours is the right amount of time for most families.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Mon–Thu 12 p.m.–8 p.m., Fri 11 a.m.–10 p.m., Sat 9 a.m.–10 p.m., Sun 9 a.m.–9 p.m. Hours shift around school breaks and holidays — confirm on the [official site](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/) or call **(410) 834-5717**.
- **Cost:** A 120-minute jump ticket is **$26.99** (single visit, all attractions). Memberships are often the better math for repeat visits: **Basic Play Monthly $26.99** (2 hours/day), **Extra Play Monthly $31.99** (unlimited weekday jump time + 2 hours weekends, with a $10.99/month parent add-on), or **Extra Play Annual $189.99** (same benefits, prepaid). See the full [tickets and memberships page](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/ways-to-play/).
- **Tickets:** [Book online](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/ways-to-play/) before you go. Walk-ups are accepted but not guaranteed during peak hours, school breaks, or party-block windows. Reserve a specific time slot.
- **Waivers:** Required for every jumper, signed by a parent or legal guardian for anyone under 18. Fill it out from the parking lot on your phone — the lobby kiosks are slow when it's busy.
- **SkySocks:** Required, purchased on-site, and reusable. Bring back a clean pair from a previous Sky Zone visit and you can skip buying new ones.
- **Parking:** Free, in the strip-mall lot. Plenty of spaces; the lot fills first thing Saturday morning when birthday parties are arriving but never gets truly tight.
- **Bathrooms:** Yes, on-site. No dedicated nursing room — quietest spot is the bench area near the toddler zone.
- **Food:** [Fuel Zone](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/) on-site sells pizza, snacks, and drinks. Outside food and drinks are not allowed (cake is generally fine for parties — confirm with the host). There's a Wegmans about ten minutes away and a Royal Farms next to the park if you need a real meal after.
- **Stroller-friendly:** The lobby is fine; the courts are not. Park the stroller along the spectator benches and use a carrier for under-2s if you're heading near the toddler zone.
- **Accessibility:** Single-level, indoor, climate-controlled. The court itself is not wheelchair-accessible, but the spectator area is. The [Sensory Time](https://www.skyzone.com/programs/sensory-hours/) program runs at participating Sky Zones with the music down or off — Columbia's participation has come and gone over the years; call to confirm before you drive.
- **Cell signal:** Strong on all major carriers. Free guest wifi in the lobby.
- **What to bring:** Water bottle (kids overheat fast), hair ties for long hair, a hoodie for the parent who's about to sit on a bench for two hours.

## Tips for families

- **The 120-minute ticket is the right one.** Sixty- and 90-minute tickets exist but kids spend the first 15 minutes figuring out where everything is, and you'll be the parent dragging out a sweaty 7-year-old who finally found the foam pit.
- **Read the membership fine print before you sign the waiver.** Some parents have ended up auto-enrolled in a monthly membership through the digital waiver flow without realizing it. Skim every checkbox; if you only want a single visit, decline the membership add-on at checkout.
- **Save the waiver in advance.** Sky Zone's [waiver](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/) is good for a year. Fill it out at home before the first visit and the lobby line moves twice as fast on every visit after.
- **Bring back your old SkySocks.** They cost a few dollars and you'll lose a pair every other visit anyway. A drawer of clean used pairs is the cheap-parent move.
- **Call ahead about closed attractions.** A recurring complaint is that one or two attractions (foam pit, climbing wall, dodgeball court) get roped off without notice. The ticket price doesn't drop when they do. A quick call before you drive saves the kid-disappointment loop.
- **Aim for opening on Saturday or Sunday.** The park opens at 9 a.m. on weekends; the first 90 minutes are calm enough that a 4-year-old can use the main court without getting bowled over. By 11 a.m., birthday parties have arrived and the open jump area is wall-to-wall.
- **Friday night is GLOW.** Music, lasers, blacklight — fun for kids 8 and up, much too much for sensory-sensitive younger kids. If your kid hates loud music or strobe-style lighting, pick a different day.
- **Use the toddler area to reset.** The fenced [Little Ninja](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/attractions/toddler-zone/) zone is the only spot where a 3-year-old won't get accidentally taken out by an 11-year-old. Even older kids occasionally retreat there when the main court gets chaotic.
- **Mix-age supervision is on you.** Court monitors are present but not always actively redirecting bigger kids out of toddler space. Plan to stand court-side with under-5s rather than from the spectator bench.
- **Two hours is the cap.** Past two hours, kids hit the wall hard. Plan a snack and a low-key follow-up — a quick stop at [Lake Elkhorn](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/recreation-parks/parks/lake-elkhorn) ten minutes away is a great cooldown if the weather cooperates.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Saturday and Sunday at opening (9 a.m.) is by far the calmest window. Weekday afternoons after 4 p.m. fill with after-school families. Weekend afternoons (11 a.m.–4 p.m.) are the loudest, with birthday parties stacked back-to-back.
- **Day of week:** Tuesday and Wednesday afternoons are the easiest weekdays. Friday GLOW is its own scene — fun for the right kids, too much for younger ones.
- **Season:** Year-round indoor, but demand spikes during winter and spring break, summer rainy days, and the holiday weeks between Christmas and New Year's. Memberships pay off fastest during school breaks.
- **Weather contingency:** This _is_ the rainy-day backup. The whole place is climate-controlled and weatherproof.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes — free, in the lot in front of the building. Plenty of spaces; the lot fills earliest on Saturday mornings when birthday parties arrive but never gets fully tight.

**How long should we plan for?**
Get the 120-minute ticket and plan for about 2.5 hours total with check-in, sock purchase, and a snack at the end. Past two hours, most kids melt down.

**What ages is this best for?**
Roughly 5 through 12. Kids 3–4 can use the toddler zone but get knocked into on the main court. Teens enjoy GLOW and dodgeball more than the open jump.

**Can I bring my own food?**
No. Outside food and drinks aren't allowed. The on-site Fuel Zone sells pizza and snacks, and birthday cakes are usually fine for parties (confirm with the party host).

**Do I need to make a reservation?**
Recommended, especially weekends, school breaks, and Friday GLOW. Walk-ups are accepted when there's space, but you can wait an hour for the next slot during peak windows.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
The lobby and Fuel Zone are stroller-passable. The trampoline courts are not — park the stroller along the benches and carry small ones in.

**Are SkySocks required?**
Yes, every jumper needs them. They're sold at the front, reusable on future visits, and worth saving in a drawer between trips.

**Is there a parent rate if I'm not jumping?**
Spectators don't pay. Bring a book — most parents end up benched for the full two hours. If you _do_ want to jump, you pay the same as your kid.

**Can babies come?**
Infants are welcome as spectators (free) but can't go on any of the trampolines. The toddler zone has a soft-play area for kids who can walk; pre-walkers will mostly be on a parent's lap.

**Is it loud?**
Yes — music, court monitor whistles, kids yelling. Bring earmuffs for noise-sensitive kids. GLOW nights are even louder.
