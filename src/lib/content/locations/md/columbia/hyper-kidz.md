# Hyper Kidz

**Address:** 9309 Snowden River Pkwy Columbia, MD 21046
**Website:** [https://columbia.hyperkidzplay.com/](https://columbia.hyperkidzplay.com/)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Indoor
**Price:** $$

## Overview

Hyper Kidz Columbia is a 16,000-square-foot ocean-themed indoor playground built for kids 0 to 13. It's all soft play and climbing — multi-level structures, tube and wave slides, a ball-blaster zone, an interactive LED dance floor, and giant light-up pin screens. There are deliberately **no arcade or coin-operated video games**; the whole place is about kids running, climbing, and burning energy. Admission is all-day and unlimited, so it works best as a destination where you settle in for a couple of hours rather than a quick stop.

The space splits into three age zones — a Mini Zone for ages 0–2, a Kid Zone for 2–7, and a Big Kid Zone for 4–13 — so toddlers aren't getting trampled by older kids. The sweet spot is roughly ages 2 through 9. Babies have their own padded corner, and 10–13s can hold their own on the big structures, but tweens tend to do a loop and lose interest. Skip it if you're sensitive to noise and chaos: weekends run loud, with several birthday parties going at once, and there are no sensory-friendly hours.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Monday–Saturday 9am–8pm, Sunday 9am–7pm. Some Sundays are blocked 10am–2pm for private events — call ahead (410-844-6841) before a Sunday-morning trip.
- **Admission (Mon–Thu):** $13.99 ages 3–13, $11.99 ages 1–2, $9.99 infants 0–11 months without a sibling.
- **Admission (Fri–Sun):** $18.99 ages 3–13, $16.99 ages 1–2, $14.99 infants 0–11 months without a sibling. Federal holidays bill at weekend rates.
- **Wonderful Wednesday:** $9.99 unlimited play for all ages, all day — the cheapest way in.
- **Adults:** Two adults are free per family with a paid child's admission. A third adult is $6.99. Infants 0–11 months get in free with a paid sibling.
- **Play time:** Unlimited, all-day open play. No timed sessions; re-entry is allowed based on capacity.
- **Grip socks required** for kids _and_ adults. Bring your own or buy a pair for $2.99 at the door.
- **Tickets:** Walk-in, no reservations needed. Every guest needs a signed waiver — fill it out online ahead of time to skip the counter line. Waivers stay valid for 36 months.
- **Parking:** Free surface lot right outside.
- **Food:** You can bring snacks for your own kids, but no other outside food or drinks (health code). The facility is **nut-free**. On-site snack counter has chips, Goldfish, fruit cups, Dippin' Dots, drinks, and coffee. Outdoor picnic tables are available.
- **What to wear:** Dress kids in leggings or gym pants/shorts. Cotton or jean shorts grip the slides and kids get stuck halfway down.
- **Indoor and climate-controlled** — a reliable rainy, hot, or freezing-day pick.

## Tips for families

- Go right at the 9am open or hit the midday lull around noon–2pm. Mid-morning is the busiest stretch, and crowds thin out when families leave for lunch and naps.
- Bring your own grip socks for everyone to skip the $2.99-per-pair counter charge — and pack spares, because socks vanish in ball pits.
- Put kids in slick play clothes (leggings, athletic shorts). Jean and cotton shorts cause friction on the slides and lead to stuck-halfway meltdowns.
- Wednesdays are the value play: $9.99 unlimited for every age, so a family of multiple kids pays a fraction of weekend pricing.
- Complete the waiver online before you leave home. With an antsy toddler in tow, you don't want to be filling out forms at the counter.
- Weekends often have several birthday parties running at once and the noise climbs fast. If your kid is sound-sensitive, aim for a weekday morning instead.
- Heads up for weekday visits: field trips and camp groups show up, sometimes several at a time. A quick call before you drive over can save you walking into a packed house.
- Lockers are available — stash your bag and a water bottle and you're free to follow your kid around the structures.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** The first hour (9–10am) is calmest. The lunch window, roughly noon to 2pm, also clears out as families head home for naps. Mid-morning is peak.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays are quieter and cheaper than weekends. Wednesdays add the $9.99 all-ages deal. Saturdays and Sundays bring the biggest crowds and the most birthday parties.
- **Season:** Fully indoor and climate-controlled, so it's a year-round option — and an especially good call when weather kills your outdoor plans.
- **Weather:** No contingency needed; the entire experience is inside.

## FAQs

**How much does it cost?**
Weekday admission (Mon–Thu) is $13.99 for ages 3–13, $11.99 for ages 1–2, and $9.99 for infants without a sibling. Weekend admission (Fri–Sun) runs $18.99, $16.99, and $14.99 for those same tiers. Two adults are free per family with a paid child; a third adult is $6.99. Wednesdays are $9.99 unlimited for all ages.

**Do I need a reservation?**
No. Hyper Kidz is walk-in, every day, with no timed tickets for open play. Every guest does need a signed waiver, which you can complete online ahead of time and which stays valid for 36 months.

**How long can we stay?**
As long as you want. Admission is unlimited all-day open play, and re-entry is allowed based on capacity, so you can leave for lunch and come back.

**What ages is it best for?**
It's built for ages 0 to 13, split into three zones (Mini 0–2, Kid 2–7, Big Kid 4–13). The sweet spot is roughly 2 to 9. Babies have a protected soft-play corner, but tweens tend to lose interest quickly.

**Are socks required?**
Yes — grip socks are mandatory for both children and adults. Bring your own or buy a pair for $2.99 at the entrance.

**Can I bring my own food?**
You can bring snacks for your own children, but no other outside food or drinks are allowed due to the health code. The facility is completely nut-free. There's an on-site snack counter and outdoor picnic tables.

**Is there parking?**
Yes — a free surface lot right outside the door.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Strollers and wheelchairs are accommodated, but staff encourage leaving strollers in the car. The play itself is climbing and crawling, so your child won't be riding in a stroller once inside.

**Is it good for a rainy or hot day?**
Yes. The whole playground is indoors and climate-controlled, which makes it a dependable backup when the weather wrecks your outdoor plans.

**Is it loud or overwhelming?**
It can be. Weekends run loud, with multiple birthday parties at once and music playing. There are no advertised sensory-friendly hours, so a weekday morning is your best bet for a calmer visit.
