## Overview

An indoor trampoline park off Oakland Mills Road with the standard Sky Zone lineup: a wall-to-wall freestyle jump court, a Foam Zone pit, SkySlam basketball dunk lanes, Ultimate Dodgeball courts, an iWall interactive climbing wall, a Speed Zone running lane, and a fenced [Little Ninja](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/attractions/toddler-zone/) toddler area for kids who aren't ready for the open court. Friday and Saturday nights run as [GLOW](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/programs/glow/) — blacklight, lasers, and loud music.

Sweet spot is **ages 5 through about 12**, with the toddler zone making it workable for under-5s if you stick to off-peak hours. Skip it if your kid is under 3 and easily overwhelmed by noise — the open court gets loud fast — or if you're expecting a single 60-minute ticket to wear out a 9-year-old. Two hours is the right amount of time for most families.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Mon–Thu 10 a.m.–8 p.m., Fri 10 a.m.–10 p.m., Sat 9 a.m.–10 p.m., Sun 9 a.m.–9 p.m. Hours shift around school breaks and holidays — confirm on the [official site](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/) or call **(410) 834-5717** before you drive.
- **Cost:** A 120-minute jump ticket is **$26.99** (single visit, all attractions). If you'll come back more than once or twice, a membership is usually the better math: **Basic Play $26.99/month** (2 hours/day), **Extra Play Monthly $31.99/month** (unlimited weekday jump time plus 2 hours on weekends, with a **$10.99/month** parent add-on), or **Extra Play Annual $189.99** prepaid. There's also a **Summer Play Pass at $49.99** (down from $69.99) good through 8/31/26. See the [tickets and memberships page](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/ways-to-play/).
- **Tickets:** [Book a time slot online](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/ways-to-play/) before you go. Walk-ups are accepted but not guaranteed during peak hours, school breaks, or party-block windows.
- **Waivers:** Required for every jumper, signed by a parent or legal guardian for anyone under 18. Fill it out from your phone in the parking lot — the lobby is slower when it's busy. The waiver is good for a year.
- **SkySocks:** The grippy Sky Zone socks are required to jump, sold on-site, and reusable. Bring a clean pair back from a past Sky Zone visit and you can skip buying new ones.
- **Parking:** Free, in the strip-mall lot out front. Plenty of spaces; it fills earliest on Saturday mornings when birthday parties arrive but rarely gets truly tight.
- **Bathrooms:** Yes, on-site. No dedicated nursing room — the quietest spot is the bench area near the toddler zone.
- **Food:** The on-site **Fuel Zone** sells pizza, snacks, and drinks. Outside food and drinks aren't allowed (birthday cake is generally fine for booked parties with prior approval — confirm with your party host). There's a Wegmans about ten minutes away and a Royal Farms next door if you want a real meal after.
- **Stroller-friendly:** The lobby and spectator area roll fine; the courts do not. Park the stroller along the benches and use a carrier for under-2s near the toddler zone.
- **Accessibility:** Single-level, indoor, climate-controlled. The spectator area is accessible; the trampoline courts themselves are not wheelchair-accessible. Sensory-friendly hours come and go at this location — call to confirm before you plan around one.
- **Cell signal / wifi:** Strong on all major carriers, plus free guest wifi in the lobby.
- **What to bring:** A water bottle (kids overheat fast on the court), hair ties for long hair, and a hoodie for the parent who's about to sit on a bench for two hours.

## Tips for families

- **Get the 120-minute ticket, not the shorter ones.** Kids burn the first 15 minutes just figuring out where everything is, and you'll be the parent prying a sweaty 7-year-old off the foam pit right as they hit their stride.
- **Decline the membership add-on at checkout if you only want one visit.** The digital waiver-and-checkout flow makes it easy to end up enrolled in a monthly membership without meaning to. Read every checkbox.
- **Fill out the waiver at home first.** It's good for a year, so doing it once before your first visit means the lobby line moves twice as fast every time after.
- **Bring back your old SkySocks.** They're a few dollars and you'll misplace a pair every other visit — a drawer of clean used pairs is the cheap-parent move.
- **Call ahead about closed attractions.** The most common recent complaint is that one or two attractions (foam pit, climbing wall, a dodgeball court) get roped off without notice, and the ticket price doesn't drop when they are. A quick call before you leave saves the kid-disappointment loop.
- **Aim for weekend opening (9 a.m. Sat/Sun).** The first 90 minutes are calm enough that a 4-year-old can use the main court without getting bowled over. By 11 a.m., birthday parties have arrived and the open jump area is wall-to-wall.
- **Friday and Saturday nights are GLOW.** Music, lasers, and blacklight — a hit for kids 8 and up, way too much for sensory-sensitive younger ones. If your kid hates loud music or flashing lights, pick a daytime slot.
- **Use the toddler zone as a reset.** The fenced [Little Ninja](https://www.skyzone.com/columbia/attractions/toddler-zone/) area is the one spot where a 3-year-old won't get accidentally taken out by an 11-year-old, and older kids sometimes retreat there when the main court gets chaotic.
- **Plan to stand court-side with under-5s.** Court monitors are present but don't always redirect bigger kids out of the toddler space, so supervision of little ones is on you — the spectator bench is too far to help.
- **Two hours is the cap.** Past that, most kids hit the wall hard. Line up a snack and a low-key follow-up — the paved [Lake Elkhorn loop](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/maryland/lake-elkhorn-trail) about ten minutes away is an easy cooldown if the weather cooperates.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Saturday and Sunday at opening (9 a.m.) is the calmest window by far. Weekday mornings are quiet during the school year but the park doesn't open until 10 a.m. Weekend afternoons (11 a.m.–4 p.m.) are the loudest, with birthday parties stacked back to back.
- **Day of week:** Tuesday and Wednesday afternoons are the easiest weekdays. Friday and Saturday evenings are GLOW — fun for the right kids, too much for younger ones.
- **Season:** Year-round and indoors, but demand spikes during winter and spring break, on summer rainy days, and in the holiday weeks between Christmas and New Year's. Memberships pay off fastest during school breaks.
- **Weather contingency:** This _is_ the rainy-day (and heat-wave) backup — the whole place is climate-controlled and weatherproof.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes — free, in the lot in front of the building. Plenty of spaces; it fills earliest on Saturday mornings when birthday parties arrive but rarely gets fully tight.

**How long should we plan for?**
Get the 120-minute ticket and plan for about 2.5 hours total once you add check-in, socks, and a snack. Past two hours, most kids melt down.

**What ages is this best for?**
Roughly 5 through 12. Kids 3–4 can use the toddler zone but get knocked into on the main court. Teens tend to prefer GLOW and dodgeball over the open jump.

**Can I bring my own food?**
No. Outside food and drinks aren't allowed. The on-site Fuel Zone sells pizza and snacks, and birthday cake is usually fine for booked parties with prior approval.

**Do I need a reservation?**
It's recommended, especially on weekends, school breaks, and GLOW nights. Walk-ups are accepted when there's space, but you can wait an hour for the next slot during peak windows.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
The lobby and spectator area are stroller-passable; the trampoline courts are not. Park the stroller along the benches and carry small ones in.

**Are SkySocks required?**
Yes, every jumper needs them. They're sold up front, reusable on future visits, and worth keeping a spare pair of between trips.

**Do I have to pay if I'm not jumping?**
Spectators don't pay to watch. If you want to jump alongside your kid, you pay the same jump price they do (or add the parent option to an Extra Play membership).

**Can babies come?**
Infants are welcome as spectators for free but can't go on the trampolines. Walking toddlers can use the soft toddler zone; pre-walkers will mostly be on a parent's lap.

**Is it loud?**
Yes — music, court-monitor whistles, and a lot of kids. Bring earmuffs for noise-sensitive kids, and know that GLOW nights are louder still.
