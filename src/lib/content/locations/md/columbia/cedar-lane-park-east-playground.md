# Cedar Lane Park East Playground

**Address:** 10745 Clarksville Pike (Route 108), Columbia, MD 21044
**Website:** [https://www.howardcountymd.gov/CedarLanePark](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/CedarLanePark)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Outdoor
**Price:** Free

## Overview

Cedar Lane Park is a 93-acre Howard County park split into two areas connected by a 0.8-mile paved path. This is the **East side**, on Route 108 — a sports-complex park with two grass multi-purpose fields, four tennis courts, basketball courts, a pavilion, restrooms, and the playground families come for. It's free, open daily, and run by [Howard County Recreation & Parks](https://www.howardcountymd.gov/CedarLanePark).

The playground suits roughly ages 2 to 12 and sits on a soft, rubberized surface. It's a solid weekday burn-off-energy stop, especially paired with the open fields for running, scootering, or kicking a ball. The big honest caveat: the play structure itself sits in full sun with very little shade over the equipment, so it's a rough pick on a hot afternoon. If you need swings or a destination playground, the East side is more of a neighborhood-grade play stop than a special outing.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Open daily, sunrise to sunset (posted as roughly 7am to dusk). No lights on the East-side playground area.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Parking:** Free lot on site. It can fill when the fields have games or tournaments; street parking is the fallback on busy weekends.
- **Restrooms:** Yes, on site, plus a water fountain.
- **Playground surface:** Rubberized/poured surface — better for crawlers and scraped knees than mulch or gravel.
- **Shade:** Shaded picnic/relief areas exist around the park, but the playground equipment is largely unshaded. Bring sunscreen and check metal/plastic surfaces for heat before letting kids climb.
- **Stroller-friendly:** Yes — paved paths and a level rubber play surface roll easily.
- **Food:** No concessions. Picnic tables and a first-come pavilion make it easy to bring your own.
- **What to bring:** Sunscreen, water, a ball or scooter for the fields, and a picnic if you're staying past an hour.

## Tips for families

- **Go on a weekday or early morning.** Weekend field bookings (especially soccer season) pack the parking lot and bring crowds of sports families; midweek mornings are calm and the playground is often empty.
- **Treat shade as the deciding factor.** The equipment bakes in afternoon sun. On warm days, come before 11am or after the structure falls into shade, and test slides with your hand first.
- **Use the fields, not just the playground.** The open grass is the East side's real strength — bring a ball, kite, or scooter. Younger kids tire of the play structure faster than at a destination playground.
- **Pack everything you'll need.** There's no concession stand. Water, snacks, and sunscreen all have to come with you.
- **Pair it with the 0.8-mile path.** The paved trail connecting the East and West areas is good for a stroller walk or a bike loop between play sessions.
- **No swings here.** If a swing set is non-negotiable for your kid, set expectations before you arrive or choose a different playground.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Early morning is best — cooler equipment, open parking, fewer crowds. Late afternoon works once the play structure falls into shade.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays are noticeably quieter. Weekends fill up when the fields are booked for games and tournaments.
- **Season:** Spring and fall are the sweet spot. The unshaded playground makes summer middays uncomfortable; winter is fine on milder days since the park stays open year-round.
- **Weather:** Fully outdoor with no rain cover at the playground — skip it in steady rain or snow. There's no on-site indoor backup.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes, there's a free lot on the East side. It can fill up when the multi-purpose fields have scheduled games, so come early on weekends or use street parking as a backup.

**Is the playground good for toddlers?**
Yes. The equipment is geared toward roughly ages 2 to 12, and the soft rubberized surface is forgiving for new walkers and climbers. There are no swings, though.

**Is there shade at the playground?**
Not much directly over the equipment — the play structure sits in open sun. The park has shaded picnic and relief areas nearby, but plan for sunscreen and check surfaces for heat on warm days.

**Are there restrooms?**
Yes, the East area has restrooms and a water fountain on site.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Yes. The paths are paved and the playground sits on a level rubberized surface, so a standard stroller rolls fine throughout.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes. There's no concession stand, but picnic tables and a first-come-first-served pavilion make it easy to bring a picnic.

**How long should we plan for?**
About 45 minutes to 1.5 hours for the playground alone. Add time if you bring a ball or scooter for the fields or walk the connecting path.

**What's the difference between the East and West areas?**
The East side (Route 108) has the playground, tennis and basketball courts, two grass fields, a pavilion, and restrooms. The West side (5081 Cedar Lane) has lighted ball diamonds, turf fields, a second playground, an indoor fitness facility, and horseshoe pits. A 0.8-mile path connects them.
