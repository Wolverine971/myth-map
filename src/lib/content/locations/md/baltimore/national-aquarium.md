## Overview

The headline Inner Harbor attraction: a multi-pier campus with the Blacktip Reef shark tank, the Atlantic Coral Reef ring, a four-story tropical rainforest under the glass pyramid, an underwater dolphin viewing window, an Australian river-gorge exhibit, the [Harbor Wetland](https://aqua.org/explore/exhibits/harbor-wetland) floating salt marsh (free, outdoor, between Piers 3 and 4), and a 4D theater. Roughly 20,000 animals across 750 species, and timed-entry tickets are the norm.

Sweet spot is **ages 3 through about 10** — kids old enough to walk the full loop on their own legs but still wowed by big animals behind glass. Skip it if your kid is under 2 and you can't comfortably wear a carrier for two hours, or if a $50 adult ticket plus $18 parking puts the trip out of budget. Strollers are not allowed on the exhibit route — that one rule trips up more first-time families than anything else.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** Mon–Thu 9 a.m.–6 p.m., **Fri 9 a.m.–9 p.m.**, Sat–Sun close between 6 p.m. and 7 p.m. depending on season. **Last entry is 60 minutes before closing.** Confirm the day-of hours on the [official visit page](https://aqua.org/visit) before driving — schedules shift in summer.
- **Cost:** Adults 21–69 **$49.95**, youth 5–20 and seniors 70+ **$39.95**, kids 4 and under **free**. Tickets are timed-entry and the aquarium does sell out on weekends and holidays — buy ahead.
- **Maryland Mornings discount:** Mon–Fri before noon, year-round, **$10 off adult / $5 off youth/senior/military** for Maryland residents with proof of residency. Blackout dates apply (most school-break weeks and major holidays) — see the [special offers page](https://aqua.org/visit/special-offers).
- **Half-Price Friday Night:** **$24.95 per person**, Fridays 5–9 p.m., year-round. The single best deal if you have older kids who can stay up — see [Half-Price Friday Night](https://aqua.org/visit/special-events/half-price-friday-night).
- **Museums for All:** Show an EBT card and photo ID for **capped admission up to 4 people** through the [Museums for All program](https://museums4all.org/).
- **Military:** **$39.95** for active duty, reserve, and veterans year-round with valid ID.
- **Parking:** $18 flat rate (24 hours) at three partner garages with validation — **Lockwood Place** (124 Market Place), **Harbor Park** (55 Market Place), and **LAZ Inner Harbor** (100 S. Gay St.). Members pay **$10**. Reserve and validate via the [parking links on the visit page](https://aqua.org/visit), or validate your gate ticket inside at Guest Services.
- **Strollers:** **Not permitted on the exhibit route.** Free stroller check is at the main entrance (space-limited). The aquarium loans **front-pack and backpack baby carriers** — ask at stroller check. Families with a child or adult who needs a stroller for mobility can pick up a wheelchair tag at stroller check.
- **Food rules:** **No outside food or drink inside.** Water bottles are fine — refill stations are throughout. Picnic areas outside on the plaza are allowed. On-site: **Pier 3 Cafe** (burgers, sandwiches, coffee), **Pier 4 Canteen** (pizza, salads), **Harbor View Room** (Level 4, Fri–Sun, bar + harbor views).
- **Re-entry:** Get your hand stamped at the information booth on the way out and you can come back the same day. Useful for a lunch break at Harborplace or a stroller break.
- **Bathrooms:** Changing tables in every restroom. **Family restroom on the ground floor of Blue Wonders.** The **Guest Relations Room** on Level 1 of Blue Wonders (behind the bubble tubes) is the private space for nursing or a quiet break.
- **Accessibility:** Free manual wheelchairs at the Main Entrance (driver's license deposit, first-come). Accessible drop-off at Gunther Circle. Designated elevators throughout. Audio tour with 22 stations and a Braille tour at the info desk. ASL interpreters with **two weeks' notice** (free admission for interpreters).
- **Sensory:** Certified [Sensory Inclusive](https://aqua.org/visit/accessibility) by KultureCity — borrow a sensory bag (noise-canceling headphones, fidgets, picture communication cards) with a photo ID. Touch bag of tactile animal items at stroller check. **First Saturday/Sunday early entry** opens 30 minutes before public opening for guests with sensory needs.

## Tips for families

- **Buy tickets online before you leave the house.** Timed entry is real — weekends and school breaks sell out, and the on-site line at the ticket window is the slowest part of the day. The [aqua.org webstore](https://cart.aqua.org/webstore/shop/viewitems.aspx?cg=nai&c=ga) is the official source.
- **Leave the stroller in the car.** This is the #1 thing parents wish they'd known. The exhibit route winds up ramps and through tight viewing nooks, and strollers are not allowed past the gate. Wear a carrier, borrow one of theirs at stroller check, or plan to carry. Kids 4 and up usually do the full loop on foot.
- **Get the parking validation.** Park at one of the three partner garages, bring the ticket inside to Guest Services for validation, and you'll pay $18 instead of the gate rate. Driving in circles looking for street parking on a Saturday is a losing game.
- **First entry of the day beats last entry by a mile.** 9 a.m. on a weekday is the calmest window. By 11 a.m. on a weekend, viewing windows are three people deep at Blacktip Reef.
- **Maryland residents: take the Maryland Mornings deal.** $10 off adults and $5 off kids/seniors before noon Monday–Friday is the cheapest legitimate way for a local family to visit. Bring ID with a Maryland address. Watch the blackout dates around school breaks.
- **Half-Price Friday Night is a real deal.** $24.95 a head from 5–9 p.m. The vibe is different — more date-night couples and locals, fewer school groups — and you usually skip the worst of the crowds in the back half of the visit.
- **Pre-feed the kids.** Outside food is banned inside. Pier 3 Cafe and Pier 4 Canteen are fine but pricey for a hungry family — a $6 small water is the usual complaint. Feed everyone before you go in or plan a re-entry lunch break.
- **Start at the top.** The recommended path takes the escalator up to Level 4 first and winds down through the rainforest. Doing it backwards means fighting upstream against the school-group flow.
- **Don't skip the Harbor Wetland.** The outdoor floating salt marsh between Piers 3 and 4 is **free** and open without an aquarium ticket — herons, terrapins, white perch, the works. It's a great kill-time spot if you arrive 30 minutes early for your entry slot.
- **Underwater Dolphin Viewing is the included one.** Anyone can stand at the window for as long as they want. The **Dolphin Tour** (12 spots, ~$30 add-on) and the **Dolphin Training Session** ($95) are separate paid experiences.
- **Borrow the sensory bag if your kid runs warm.** KultureCity bags with noise-canceling headphones, fidgets, and picture cards are at Guest Relations — ID-checkout, not advertised on signage. The viewing tanks themselves are dim and quiet, but the lobby, escalators, and 4D theater can be a lot.
- **Plan for 2.5 to 4 hours.** Most families burn out around 3 hours. Tweens move faster; toddlers melt down sooner. Use the hand-stamp re-entry to bail out for lunch and come back if you have older kids who want more.
- **Buy 4D Theater tickets only if it's the right kid.** The shows are an add-on, **not included** with admission. Younger kids love Sea Lion 4D; older kids prefer Shark. Shows run ~20 minutes and capacity is high enough that walk-up is fine.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** First entry of the day (9 a.m.) on a weekday or Half-Price Friday Night after 5 p.m. Weekend afternoons after 3 p.m. clear out as the early-morning timed slots cycle through.
- **Day of week:** Tuesday–Thursday during the school year is consistently quietest. Fridays after 5 p.m. and Saturdays at open are second-best. Weekends midday and any day in July are the worst.
- **Season:** Year-round indoor. Off-peak windows are mid-September through early November and the middle of January. Spring break weeks (DC and Baltimore schools, late March through early April) are nearly as crowded as summer.
- **Weather contingency:** This is the canonical Baltimore rainy-day, hot-day, or snow-day move. The outdoor Harbor Wetland is the only weather-dependent piece, and it's a short walk.

## FAQs

**Is there parking?**
Yes — three partner garages within a few blocks (Lockwood Place, Harbor Park, LAZ Inner Harbor) validate to $18 for the day, or $10 for members. Bring your parking gate ticket to Guest Services or pre-pay online before you go.

**How long should we plan for?**
Two and a half to four hours for most families. Add an hour if you're doing the 4D theater or a paid dolphin experience. The hand-stamp re-entry lets you break for lunch and come back.

**What ages is this best for?**
Roughly 3 to 10 is the sweet spot. Under-2s are free but the no-stroller rule means you'll be carrying the whole time. Kids 11+ are still engaged but move through faster — a Half-Price Friday Night visit is a better fit for tweens than a full-price daytime ticket.

**Can I bring my own food?**
No outside food or drink inside the aquarium buildings. Water bottles are fine and refill stations are everywhere. Picnic outside on the plaza or use the same-day re-entry hand stamp to eat at Harborplace.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
No — strollers are not allowed on the exhibit route. Free stroller check is by the main entrance (space-limited), and the aquarium loans front and back baby carriers. Families whose child or caregiver needs a stroller for mobility reasons can request a wheelchair tag.

**Is there a place to nurse or change a baby?**
Yes. Changing tables are in every restroom, and there's a family restroom on the ground floor of Blue Wonders. The Guest Relations Room on Level 1 of Blue Wonders is a private, quiet space for nursing or a sensory break.

**Do I need a reservation?**
Yes — admission is timed-entry, and weekends sell out. Book ahead on the [aqua.org webstore](https://cart.aqua.org/webstore/shop/viewitems.aspx?cg=nai&c=ga). Walk-up tickets are sometimes available on quiet weekdays but it's risky to count on it.

**Are there sensory-friendly options?**
Yes — KultureCity sensory bags at Guest Relations (photo ID checkout), a downloadable social story, a quiet Guest Relations Room on Level 1 of Blue Wonders, and First Saturday/Sunday early entry 30 minutes before public opening. See the [accessibility page](https://aqua.org/visit/accessibility).

**Is the 4D theater included?**
No — it's an extra fee on top of admission. Shows rotate (Shark, Sea Lion, Turtle Odyssey are typical) and run about 20 minutes.

**What if I'm running late for my timed entry?**
You can usually still get in, depending on capacity and the queue at the door. Don't push it more than 30–45 minutes — the line for late entries is its own waiting game on busy days.

**Is the dolphin show worth it?**
There's no choreographed dolphin "show" — the dolphins live in Dolphin Discovery and you can watch through the underwater viewing window for as long as you want with regular admission. The paid Dolphin Tour and Dolphin Training Session are smaller, on-deck experiences with extra cost.
