**Setting:** Outdoor
**Price:** $

## Overview

A 300-acre lake wrapped in 550 acres of Carroll County woods and fields, with boat rentals, floating fishing piers, two playgrounds, five-plus miles of trails, and a free nature center housing live reptiles and rescued hawks and owls. It's the closest thing south Carroll County has to a full-day outdoor basecamp: rent a pedal boat, walk the lake loop, let the kids climb, and picnic under the trees — all for one per-vehicle gate fee.

One big expectation to set before you drive over: **there is no swimming**. Piney Run Lake is a reservoir, and swimming and wading are prohibited — for kids and dogs alike. If your crew hears "lake" and pictures a beach day, this is not that trip. Skip it on rainy days too; aside from the small nature center, everything here is outdoors.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** March 1 – November 30, open daily 7:00 am to sunset. December through February, open Monday–Friday 7:00 am – 4:00 pm only — closed weekends and holidays in winter.
- **Cost:** $6 per vehicle for Carroll County residents, $12 per vehicle for everyone else. Seniors free. Active-duty military, veterans, and their spouses and dependents free with ID. Season pass: $75 county / $100 out-of-county.
- **Boat rentals:** Canoes, kayaks, and rowboats are $17/hour or $65/day; pedal boats are $30/hour. Life jackets are provided and required for everyone in a rental boat. **No child under 4 is allowed in rental boats**, and the renter must be 18+.
- **Bringing your own boat:** $10 launch fee. Electric motors only on the lake — no gas motors running. Paddleboards are allowed.
- **Fishing:** Anyone 16 and older needs a [Maryland fishing license](https://dnr.maryland.gov/fisheries/pages/hotspots/pineyrun.aspx); kids under 16 fish free. Floating piers and shoreline access near the boat launch. Nightcrawlers are sold in the park during the season.
- **Nature center:** Free. Open March–November Wednesday–Saturday 11 am – 5 pm, Sunday noon – 5 pm, closed Monday–Tuesday. Winter: weekdays only, 11 am – 4 pm.
- **Bathrooms:** Near the boathouse and the playground on the west shore of the lake. There are no restrooms on the east shore — plan bathroom stops before a trail loop.
- **Strollers:** Paved roads and the playground areas roll fine. The dirt trails have tree roots and a few steep pitches — bring a carrier for babies instead.
- **Dogs:** Allowed on leash, and even welcome on rental boats. They cannot swim in the lake.
- **Picnics:** Tables, grills, and six rentable pavilions ($200–$325/day for parties). Bringing your own food is the norm here — there's no restaurant, so pack a cooler.

## Tips for families

- Make the [nature center](https://www.carrollcountymd.gov/government/directory/recreation-parks/places-to-go/piney-run-park/piney-run-nature-center/) your anchor stop — it's free, staffed by naturalists, and the live reptiles plus the aviary of rescued hawks and owls are the part kids talk about afterward. Just don't come Monday or Tuesday, when it's closed.
- The 1.1-mile [Lake Trail loop](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/maryland/piney-run-lake-trail) is the right length for preschool legs — about 30–40 minutes at kid pace with water views the whole way. Hikers regularly spot deer, and the raptor cages are visible from the trail.
- Traveling with a baby plus a bigger kid? The under-4 boat ban means one adult boats while the other works the playground — split up rather than skipping the lake entirely.
- Kids under 16 don't need a fishing license, the floating piers are easy for small casters, and bluegill bite fast enough to hold a 5-year-old's attention. Piney Run is rated one of the best fisheries in Maryland, so this is a legitimately good first-fishing-trip spot.
- Summer weekends get crowded around the boat ramp and boathouse, and the bathrooms strain at peak. A weekday morning gets you the same park with a fraction of the people.
- Boat rentals don't run every day the park gates are open, especially early and late in the season. Call the boathouse at 410-795-6385 before you promise anyone a pedal boat.
- No swimming means hot afternoons get rough. On 90-degree days, do Piney Run in the morning and finish at the Splash Pad of Sykesville about ten minutes away.
- One of the playgrounds is wheelchair-friendly with genuinely good slides — reviewers with mobility needs call it out as a highlight.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Mornings, especially in summer — cooler for the trail, calmer water for the boats, and you beat the boat-ramp crowd.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays win big here. Summer Saturdays bring picnickers, kayakers, and pavilion parties all funneling through the same west-shore area.
- **Season:** Late spring and early fall are the sweet spot — boat rentals running, trails comfortable, crowds manageable. Summer works with a morning start. Winter is trails-only, weekdays-only, so most families skip December through February.
- **Weather contingency:** Almost everything is outdoors. The nature center is a fine 45-minute stop but not enough to save a rainy day — reschedule instead.

## FAQs

**How much does it cost to get in?**
$6 per vehicle if you live in Carroll County, $12 per vehicle if you don't. Seniors and military families with ID get in free. The fee covers everyone in the car — playgrounds, trails, and nature center included.

**Can we swim in the lake?**
No. Piney Run Lake is a reservoir and swimming and wading are prohibited — no exceptions for hot days, and dogs can't swim either. Set this expectation with kids before you arrive.

**Do kids need a fishing license?**
Not until they turn 16. Adults 16+ need a Maryland fishing license plus the park entrance fee. Nightcrawlers are sold in the park during the season.

**Can toddlers go on the boats?**
Children under 4 are not allowed in rental boats, full stop. Kids 4 and up ride with a life jacket on (provided by the boathouse). The renter has to be at least 18.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Partially. Paved roads, the playground areas, and the area around the boathouse work fine. The dirt hiking trails have roots and a few steep sections — a carrier is the better call for the lake loops.

**Are dogs allowed?**
Yes, on leash, and they're welcome on rental boats too. Clean-up rules are enforced, and they cannot go in the water.

**Is the park open in winter?**
Weekdays only — Monday through Friday, 7 am to 4 pm, December through February. The gates are closed on winter weekends and holidays, so save it for a school break day.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes — picnicking is the standard move here. There are tables, grills, and pavilions near the playgrounds. No restaurant on site, so pack what you need.
