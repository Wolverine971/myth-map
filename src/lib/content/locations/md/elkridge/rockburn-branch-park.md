## Overview

Rockburn Branch Park is a roughly 415-acre Howard County park split into two sections a few minutes apart, and the two sides do different jobs. The West Area (6105 Rockburn Branch Park Road) is the family side: a two-structure playground, a free 18-hole disc golf course, tennis and basketball courts, and picnic pavilions. The East Area (5400 Landing Road) is the sports-and-wheels side, with ball fields, synthetic turf fields, and a BMX skills track. Around 9 miles of trails thread through the woods, marsh, and stream valley that connect the two, and the network links into Patapsco Valley State Park if you want to keep walking.

It's a strong pick for families who want a playground plus room to roam without paying for anything — everything here is free. Skip it as a rainy-day plan, because it's all outdoors with no indoor backup, and go in knowing the shade right at the playground is spotty, so midday in July can be rough.

## What to know before you go

- **Hours:** 7 a.m. to dusk, or as posted. Lit fields sometimes stay open later for evening games.
- **Cost:** Free — entry, parking, the playground, the trails, and disc golf all cost nothing.
- **Two sections, two addresses:** The West Area (6105 Rockburn Branch Park Rd) holds the playground, disc golf, and tennis/basketball courts. The East Area (5400 Landing Road) has the ball fields, turf fields, and the BMX/bike skills track. Put the right one in your GPS.
- **Parking:** Free paved lots at both sections. They fill fast on weekend tournament days; a weekday morning, you'll have your pick.
- **Restrooms:** Permanent restrooms sit near the ball fields; there are porta-potties closer to the playground and disc golf course. Changing-table availability isn't posted anywhere official.
- **Food:** A concession stand runs at the fields during games and season. Otherwise bring your own — picnic tables and pavilions are first-come unless reserved.
- **Pavilions:** Reservable April 1–November 30 (call 410-313-4682 or book online); free on a first-come basis for groups under 20 the rest of the year.
- **Strollers:** The playground area and paved paths roll fine; the natural-surface woodland trails and a few disc-golf bridges do not.
- **Dogs:** Welcome on a leash — clean up after them.
- **Rules:** No alcohol, and no smoking or tobacco of any kind (all Howard County parks are tobacco-free).
- **Bring:** Water and sun hats, a picnic blanket or pop-up shade, a set of discs if you want to try the course, and bug spray in summer near the marshy trail sections.

## Tips for families

- The playground has two separate structures — a toddler area and a bigger-kid setup — plus swings including one accessible swing, so a spread of ages can play at the same time.
- Shade is hit or miss right at the play area. Claim a shaded picnic table early or bring a pop-up canopy if you're staying through midday in summer.
- Come on a weekday morning if you can. Weekend game days, especially in spring and fall, pack the lots and bring noise from the fields.
- The 18-hole disc golf course is free, wooded, and only moderately hilly — a good first course for grade-schoolers and new players. Bring your own discs; there's no rental on site.
- Skip the far disc-golf holes with a stroller: the bridges on holes 8 and 9 have about a 3-inch step, and a couple of hole-to-hole transitions are steep.
- Restrooms are over by the ball fields, not the playground. Factor the walk in with newly potty-trained kids, or use the porta-potties near the play area.
- For a nature walk instead of playground time, the West Area has more open sky (better for spotting birds) plus some paved path, while the East Area has the more mature woods. Over 170 bird species have been logged here.
- The trail network connects to Patapsco Valley State Park, so a quick playground stop can turn into a real hike if the kids still have energy.

## Best time to visit

- **Time of day:** Weekday mornings are the calmest and coolest. Late afternoons and evenings get louder and busier when field games run under the lights.
- **Day of week:** Weekdays beat weekends by a wide margin for parking and quiet. Saturdays during sports season are the busiest stretch.
- **Season:** Spring and fall are the sweet spot — comfortable temperatures, active birding, and leafed-out trees that do the shade work the playground doesn't. Summer is pleasant early but hot and buggy near the marsh by midday. Winter walks and disc golf still work if you bundle up.
- **Weather:** There's no indoor backup. Steady rain washes out the visit and turns the natural trails muddy, though the paved paths and disc-golf tees dry quickly afterward.

## FAQs

**Is there parking, and is it free?**
Yes. There are free paved lots at both the West Area (6105 Rockburn Branch Park Rd) and the East Area (5400 Landing Road). They fill up on weekend game days, so arrive early or come on a weekday.

**Which entrance has the playground?**
The playground, disc golf, tennis, and basketball are all in the West Area at 6105 Rockburn Branch Park Road. The East Area on Landing Road is ball fields, turf fields, and the BMX track.

**How long should we plan for?**
A playground-only stop runs about an hour. Add the disc golf course (about 1.5 to 2 hours) or a trail walk and you can fill a half day.

**Is it stroller-friendly?**
Partly. The playground area and paved paths roll fine, but the natural woodland trails and a few disc-golf bridges (with a small step) do not.

**Are there bathrooms and changing tables?**
There are permanent restrooms near the ball fields and porta-potties near the playground and disc golf. Changing-table availability isn't posted, so bring a portable changing pad to be safe.

**Can we bring our own food?**
Yes. Picnic tables and pavilions are open first-come unless reserved, and a concession stand runs at the fields during games. Pavilions can be reserved from April through November.

**Is the disc golf course good for beginners?**
Yes. It's 18 free holes with gentle hills and a mix of open and wooded fairways — a solid first course for kids and new players. Bring your own discs.

**Are dogs allowed?**
Yes, on a leash, as long as you clean up after them.

**Is it worth it on a hot day?**
Only early. The playground and fields get full sun, there's no water play or AC, and the marshy trail sections draw bugs. The wooded trails stay cooler, but plan for morning.
