## Overview

South Tract is the family-oriented half of Patuxent Research Refuge: a free indoor wildlife center surrounded by five miles of walking trails, two lakes, pollinator gardens, and an easy paved Story Trail. The [National Wildlife Visitor Center](https://www.fws.gov/refuge/patuxent-research/patuxent-visitor-center) gives younger kids something concrete to do before or after a walk, including wildlife exhibits, films, self-guided activities, a bird blind, and 35-minute Kids Discovery Center sessions for ages 3–10.

The best first visit is simple: arrive while the center is open, spend 30–60 minutes indoors, then choose the paved 0.3-mile Loop Trail for little legs or the 1.5-mile Cash Lake Trail for a longer walk with lake overlooks and floating walkways. This is a wildlife refuge, not a playground. Skip it if your kids need climbing equipment, bikes, or constant entertainment; come when they are happy to look for turtles, birds, bugs, and animal tracks.

## What to know before you go

- **Grounds hours:** Open sunrise to sunset Monday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, and Sunday. On Tuesday, the grounds close at 4 p.m. The refuge is closed on federal holidays.
- **Visitor center hours:** Wednesday–Saturday, 10 a.m.–4 p.m.; closed on federal holidays. The outdoor trails are still usable on other open days, but the indoor exhibits, staffed help, and bookstore will not be available.
- **Cost:** Admission, parking, and public programs are free. Fishing requires a Maryland non-tidal license for everyone 16 and older.
- **Reservations:** General visits do not require a reservation. The Kids Discovery Center runs 35-minute sessions at 10, 11, and noon, Wednesday–Saturday. Call (301) 497-5772 before your visit to reserve.
- **Parking:** Follow Scarlet Tanager Loop about 1.4 miles from the Powder Mill Road entrance to the main visitor-center lot. Anglers can also use the Cash Lake entrance on Route 197 when that seasonal fishing area is open. Parking passes are no longer required.
- **Bathrooms:** The official trail map marks restrooms beside the paved Loop Trail near the visitor-center parking area. Plan around the visitor-center schedule if you prefer indoor facilities.
- **Food:** Nothing is sold on the refuge, so bring water and snacks. The visitor center's back patio is used as a picnic area; pack out everything you bring.
- **Strollers:** The paved 0.3-mile Loop Trail and paved route to the bird blind are the easiest choices. Cash Lake and the forest trails use natural surfaces and floating walkways, so a jogging stroller or carrier is the safer bet there.
- **Mobility access:** The paved Loop Trail and nearby bird blind work for wheels and limited-mobility visitors. The rest of the trail network is mixed natural terrain.
- **Dogs:** Leashed dogs are welcome on refuge trails. The leash must be 6 feet or shorter and held in hand. Only service animals may enter buildings.
- **Bikes:** South Tract trails are foot-traffic only. Bikes are not permitted on them.
- **What to bring:** Water, snacks, binoculars, bug spray, closed shoes, and a carrier if your stroller is not built for trails.

## Tips for families

- Visit Wednesday through Saturday and arrive close to 10 a.m. That gives you the indoor center as a weather and bathroom backup, then plenty of daylight for a trail.
- For ages 3–10, call ahead for the Kids Discovery Center. A parent stays and works through the activities with the child; the room is not baby-proofed and contains small pieces, so it is not suitable for children under 3.
- Start toddlers and preschoolers on the 0.3-mile paved Loop Trail. The seasonal Story Trail turns the walk into a page-by-page scavenger hunt and gets you back to the parking lot before attention spans collapse.
- Save the 1.5-mile Cash Lake Trail for kids who can comfortably walk for an hour or more. The lake overlooks and floating walkways give the route natural stopping points, but there is no quick indoor escape once you are on the far side.
- Check the bird blind just outside the visitor center's trail door. The viewing openings work at different heights, the approach is paved, and the feeders give impatient kids a better chance of seeing birds up close.
- Look for the Family Fun area in the hallway to the right of the reception desk. Its games and crafts are available for self-guided use, with volunteers staffing it on selected dates rather than every day.
- Bring lunch instead of expecting a cafe or vending machine. Eat on the back patio, then do the short loop; food is less useful once everyone is committed to Cash Lake.
- Do a tick check after any forest trail, especially in late spring and summer. Keep kids on marked paths, use repellent, and skip sandals.
- Do not promise a wildlife sighting. Turn the walk into a checklist—bird call, turtle, feather, frog, dragonfly—so a quiet lake still feels like a win.

## Best time to visit

- **Best day:** Wednesday–Saturday is the full experience because the visitor center is open from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Sunday works for trails, but the indoor center is closed.
- **Best time:** Arrive at 10 a.m. for exhibits or a reserved discovery session, then walk before afternoon heat builds. For wildlife watching alone, the grounds at sunrise are quieter and more active.
- **Best season:** Spring and fall bring comfortable walking weather, migrating birds, and fewer biting insects than midsummer. Summer adds busy pollinator gardens, dragonflies, and turtles, but also humidity and ticks.
- **Rain or extreme temperatures:** On Wednesday–Saturday, the visitor center makes a worthwhile indoor-only visit and the paved Story Trail can be a short add-on between showers. On days when the building is closed, there is no real weather backup.
- **Winter:** The indoor exhibits and short paved loop still work well. Cash Lake is quieter and more exposed once the leaves are down, so dress for wind near the water.

## FAQs

**Is Patuxent Research Refuge South Tract free?**
Yes. There is no admission or parking charge, and the refuge's public programs are free. Bring money only for bookstore purchases or a fishing license if an angler age 16 or older needs one.

**How long should we plan to stay?**
Allow 60–90 minutes for the visitor center and paved Loop Trail. Add another 60–90 minutes if your family wants to walk the 1.5-mile Cash Lake Trail.

**Is the visitor center open every day?**
No. It is open Wednesday–Saturday from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. The grounds are open sunrise to sunset most days, with a 4 p.m. Tuesday closing, and the entire refuge closes on federal holidays.

**Do we need a reservation for the Kids Discovery Center?**
Call (301) 497-5772 before you go. Sessions begin at 10, 11, and noon and last 35 minutes. The program is designed for ages 3–10, and an adult participates with the child.

**Is South Tract stroller-friendly?**
Partly. The 0.3-mile Loop Trail and bird-blind approach are paved. Use a jogging stroller or carrier for the natural-surface lake and forest trails.

**Are there bathrooms?**
Yes. The official South Tract map marks restrooms beside the Loop Trail near the main parking area. The visitor center is only open Wednesday–Saturday, so plan accordingly on other days.

**Can we bring food?**
Yes. Nothing is sold at the refuge, and the visitor center's back patio is used for picnics. Bring your own food and water and pack out your trash.

**Can kids fish at Cash Lake?**
Yes, during the April–September season. Children 15 and younger do not need a Maryland fishing license; older anglers do. Fishing is allowed from the shore and pier, not from the floating trail bridges, and lead sinkers are prohibited.

**Are dogs allowed?**
Dogs are allowed on trails with a hand-held leash no longer than 6 feet. Only service animals may enter the visitor center.
