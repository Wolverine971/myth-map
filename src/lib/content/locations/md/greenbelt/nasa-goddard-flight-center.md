# NASA Goddard Flight Center

**Address:** 8800 Greenbelt Road Greenbelt, MD 20771
**Website:** [https://www.nasa.gov/goddard/visitor-center/](https://www.nasa.gov/goddard/visitor-center/)
**Type:** Activity
**Setting:** Indoor, Outdoor
**Price:** Free

## Overview

NASA Goddard Flight Center is one of NASA’s major space research and engineering campuses — and the Visitor Center is a fun, free way to spark kids’ curiosity about rockets, satellites, and space science. Expect hands-on exhibits, big “wow” objects, and occasional special events that can turn a quick stop into a half-day adventure.

## What to know before you go

- **Visitor access varies.** Some areas of the campus have security restrictions. Use the Visitor Center as your main destination unless you have confirmed access for a tour or event.
- **Bring an ID.** If you’re attending an event or tour that requires check-in, everyone in your party may need identification (rules can change).
- **Stroller-friendly.** Indoor exhibit spaces are typically easier with strollers than crowded outdoor events; bring a light stroller if you’ll be walking a lot.
- **Food plan:** Pack snacks and water (especially for kids). Treat any on-site food as a bonus, not a guarantee.
- **Quiet kits help.** A small set of headphones, fidgets, or a favorite book can make the visit smoother for kids who need breaks.

## Tips for families

- **Start with the biggest “wow” item.** Let kids burn off their excitement, then circle back to smaller exhibits.
- **Make it a scavenger hunt.** Look for words like “satellite,” “robot,” “Mars,” and “moon” to keep preschoolers engaged.
- **Talk in “kid scale.”** “This helps us take pictures of Earth” lands better than “Earth observation instrumentation.”
- **Pair with a playground stop.** If your crew needs to run after indoor time, plan a nearby park for after.

## Best time to visit

- **Weekday mornings** tend to be calmer (school groups can change that quickly).
- **Hot or rainy days** are great for indoor exhibits.
- If you’re aiming for a **special event**, confirm details on the Visitor Center page before you go.

## FAQs

### Is it really free?

The Visitor Center is typically free to visit, but special events or tours may have their own requirements. Check the Visitor Center page before you go.

### Can we see rockets or spacecraft?

You can usually count on space-themed exhibits and large display pieces, but what’s on view can change. The Visitor Center is the best place to start for kid-friendly displays.

### Is this good for toddlers?

Yes — shorter visits work well. Plan on a “one-hour win,” with snacks and a simple scavenger hunt.

### Do we need tickets or reservations?

For a normal Visitor Center visit you may not, but tours/events sometimes do. Confirm ahead of time on the Visitor Center page.
