# Tiny Tribe Adventures — Scrappy Mode

> Companion to [promotion-strategy.md](./promotion-strategy.md). That doc is the version of this project after it has traction. **This doc is for right now.**

## Reality check

- It's one person (DJ) with a side project, not a team with a brand.
- ~57 locations across ~19 MD cities. Real, useful, but small.
- Emails go out from a **personal Gmail**, not `hello@tinytribeadventures.com`.
- There is no press story. There is no partnership program. Both of those words are bigger than the project.
- The goal right now is not "land coverage" or "secure partnerships." It's: **get a handful of real parents to open it, click around, and tell one friend.**

If 30 parents in MD actually use the map next Saturday, that's a win. That's the bar.

## What to do instead of the formal plan

The formal plan (press kit, sending domain, segmented templates, 57 personalized partnership emails) is the right plan **later**. It is the wrong plan this week. Don't pre-build infrastructure for traction that doesn't exist yet.

Scrappy version, in order of "actually do it tomorrow":

### 1. Tell people you already know

The single highest-conversion channel for a side project is **people who already like you.**

- Text 5–10 parent friends: "hey I made a little thing for finding stuff to do with kids in MD — would you take a look and tell me if it's useful? `tinytribeadventures.com`"
- Same message to the parents at your kid's school, the families on the block, the people in your group chat.
- This isn't "marketing." It's showing your friends something you made.

Goal: 10–20 sent. Aim for 3–5 real opens of the site. One person who actually uses it Saturday.

### 2. Post in groups you're already in

- The MD/Baltimore/DC parent Facebook groups you're a member of.
- The neighborhood group, the school parent group, the local moms-of-toddlers group.
- Nextdoor, if you're on it.

Tone: **not pitching, sharing.** Something like:

> "I got tired of googling 'things to do with kids near me' so I made a little map of family-friendly spots around Baltimore/MD — `tinytribeadventures.com`. Mostly places we've actually taken our kids. Feedback welcome, and tell me what I'm missing."

Critical: post as **DJ the parent**, not "Tiny Tribe Adventures." People mute brands. They engage with people.

### 3. Drop it in 1–2 relevant Reddit threads per week

- `r/baltimore`, `r/Maryland`, `r/howardcountymd`, parenting subs for the region.
- Don't make a self-promo post. **Wait for someone to ask** "where can I take my kids this weekend?" and reply with 2–3 actual suggestions plus a link.
- Helpful first, link second. If the comment is useful even without the link, it'll survive.

### 4. Hand it to people in the wild

This is the one that actually compounds.

- At the playground, on a hike, at the museum: "oh you have kids about that age, I made this little site for finding stuff to do, take a look."
- Literally show them on your phone. Watch what they tap on. That's free user research and free distribution at the same time.

### 5. Email a few locations you actually love — as yourself

This is the tiny version of the partnership plan. Don't send 57. Send 3.

- Pick 3 locations on the site that you actually personally love and have visited multiple times.
- Email the venue (whatever address is on their site) from your personal Gmail.
- One paragraph. No pitch deck, no "partnership opportunity."

> Subject: Took my kids to [place] last month
>
> Hey — I'm a dad in [town] and we've been to [place] a bunch with our kids. I run a tiny side project at tinytribeadventures.com where I'm cataloging the spots that actually work for families in MD, and you're on it (here: [link]). No ask, just wanted to say thanks and let you know we send you a trickle of traffic. If you ever want to share it with your visitors, that'd be cool. Either way, see you next visit.
>
> DJ

That's it. If 1 of 3 replies, you're winning. If 0 of 3 reply, no harm done.

## What to NOT do right now

- ❌ Don't set up `hello@tinytribeadventures.com`, SPF, DKIM, DMARC. You're sending from Gmail.
- ❌ Don't write segmented templates by venue type. You're sending 3 emails, not 57.
- ❌ Don't pitch press. There is no story yet.
- ❌ Don't build UTM dashboards. You'll know it's working when your wife says "someone at school mentioned the site."
- ❌ Don't make it sound like a company. It's a side project. That's a feature, not a bug — parents trust other parents, not brands.
- ❌ Don't polish the `/press` page hoping it'll attract press. It's there if anyone asks, fine. Don't optimize it.

## When to graduate to the formal plan

Switch to [promotion-strategy.md](./promotion-strategy.md) when **any one** of these is true:

- 100+ weekly visitors from word-of-mouth alone (proves the thing works).
- 2+ locations have already mentioned the site on their own, unprompted (proves partnerships are warm).
- You've personally outgrown answering the inbox from Gmail (good problem to have).
- You decide to take it seriously as more than a side project.

Until then: scrappy.

## Success metric for the next 30 days

One number: **how many parents you know personally have actually opened the site at least once.**

If that number is 15 by mid-June, the project is healthier than 90% of side projects at this stage. Everything else follows from there.
